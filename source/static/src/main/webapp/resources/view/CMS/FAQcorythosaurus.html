Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Corythosaurus?

The Natural History Museum lists length about 10.0 m. Other published figures commonly fall about 7.7-9 m long and about 2.5-3.8 tonnes. Turn on the human figure to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

What was the crest for?

The rounded bony crest is shaped like a Corinthian helmet (the name Corythosaurus). Like other lambeosaurine crests, it housed nasal passages; proposed roles include sound resonance and display. No single function is proven, and this page does not claim one.

When and where did it live?

In the Late Cretaceous, about 76 to 74 million years ago, in what is now Canada and the USA. Barnum Brown named Corythosaurus casuarius in 1914.

How is it different from Parasaurolophus?

Both are crested duckbills, but Corythosaurus has a tall, rounded helmet crest, while Parasaurolophus is known for a long, hollow tube crest sweeping back from the skull. This page focuses on the helmet-crested form.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. Length, weight, and age figures are real published values, with ranges cited because sources vary.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
