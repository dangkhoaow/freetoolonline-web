How the Coelophysis size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Coelophysis model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 2.5 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Coelophysis

What a person would see is a slender early bipedal theropod whose panel length is about 2.5 m - a little longer than one body length of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 0.8 m high in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the snout to feel how a small Late Triassic carnivore compares to you.


What the Coelophysis size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays the disclosed about 15-25 kg range with panel baseline 20 kg.
