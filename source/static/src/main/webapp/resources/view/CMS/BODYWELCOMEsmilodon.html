Smilodon (Sabre-tooth Cat) 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

This page renders a Smilodon as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click its head, a leg, or its sabre teeth for a fact. Smilodon was a mammal, not a dinosaur.

Smilodon fatalis stood roughly 1 m tall at the shoulder, about 2.5 m long, and weighed near 280 kg - close to a modern lion but more heavily built. Its upper canines grew up to about 28 cm long, the feature behind the sabre-tooth cat name; despite the popular nickname "sabre-tooth tiger," it was not closely related to tigers or any living big cat.

It lived across the Americas from about 2.5 million to roughly 10,000 years ago, well after the dinosaurs died out. First described in 1841 from a Brazilian fossil by Danish naturalist Peter Wilhelm Lund, thousands of skeletons pulled from the La Brea Tar Pits in Los Angeles give it an unusually deep fossil record among Ice Age predators.
