How to View Tsintaosaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Tsintaosaurus 3D Viewer runs a Late Cretaceous Shandong lambeosaurine with an upright nasal crest - length about 8.3-12 m (Size vs human uses 10 m), mass about 2.5-3 tonnes. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body part for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Tsintaosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Tsintaosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Tsintaosaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural crested hadrosaur silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Tsintaosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Tsintaosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle breathing and leg sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest length ratio used by the viewer (10 m representative within the published about 8.3-12 m range; Wikipedia about 8.3 m; NHM lists 12.0 m)

Read the Tsintaosaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Tsintaosaurus facts panel lists length about 8.3-12 m (Wikipedia about 8.3 m; NHM lists 12.0 m; Size vs human uses 10 m), hip height about 3.6 m for a large adult, mass about 2.5-3 tonnes commonly cited (Wikipedia about 2.5 t), the Late Cretaceous window about 84-71 million years ago in Shandong (Jingangkou Formation), herbivore diet, and naming by Young in 1958 (type species T. spinorhinus). Distinct from Parasaurolophus (long tubular crest) and Edmontosaurus (no bony crest). Older unicorn-spike art versus newer lobate-crest reconstructions (e.g. Prieto-Marquez and Wagner 2013) means crest soft-tissue outline beyond the preserved nasal process is interpretive. A short click - not a drag - on head, body, leg, or crest surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction; when the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Tsintaosaurus how-to guide is not

The Tsintaosaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the disclosed about 8.3-12 m / 2.5-3 t ranges, does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, does not invent game score or win states, and does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
