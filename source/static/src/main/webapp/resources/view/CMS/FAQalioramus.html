Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Alioramus?

Kurzanov (1976) estimated about 5-6 m for Alioramus remotus. Molina-Perez and Larramendi (2016) give about 5.5 m and 500 kg for A. remotus, and about 5 m and 385 kg for A. altai. Paul (1988) listed about 6 m and 700 kg. This page uses 5.5 m and 500 kg as facts-panel baselines and notes the range. Turn on the human figure to compare with a 1.8 m person.

What kind of dinosaur was Alioramus?

It is a tyrannosaurid theropod in the tribe Alioramini - a long-snouted branch separate from Tarbosaurus and Tyrannosaurus. Specimens show a slender build and distinctive nasal crests or bumps along the snout.

When and where did Alioramus live?

In Late Cretaceous rocks of the Nemegt Formation in Mongolia, about 72-66 million years ago. Sergei Kurzanov named Alioramus remotus in 1976; Brusatte and colleagues named A. altai in 2009 from a more complete skeleton.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited where sources disagree.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
