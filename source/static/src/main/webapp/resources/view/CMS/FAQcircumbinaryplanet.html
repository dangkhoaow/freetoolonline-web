Frequently Asked Questions

What is a circumbinary planet?

A planet that orbits both stars of a binary system at once, rather than orbiting just one of them.

Is Kepler-16b really like Tatooine?

It really does orbit two stars, but it is a cold gas giant with no solid surface, unlike the fictional desert world - so there is no ground to stand on and watch the double sunset.

How was Kepler-16b discovered?

NASA's Kepler space telescope, confirmed in 2011, caught the planet transiting both stars in turn on top of the two stars already eclipsing each other, giving unusually precise measurements of every body's size and mass.

How far apart are the two stars?

About 0.23 AU on average, orbiting each other every 41 days on a noticeably eccentric path.

How far out does the planet orbit?

About 0.70 AU from the stars' shared center of mass, completing one orbit every 228.8 days, similar to Venus's distance from the Sun.

How big is Kepler-16b?

About three-quarters of Jupiter's radius and a third of its mass, making it comparable in size to Saturn.

Is this scene to scale?

No. Star and planet sizes are enlarged relative to their orbits for visibility, and the motion is heavily sped up.
