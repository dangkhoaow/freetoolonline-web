Acrocanthosaurus 3D Viewer

This page renders an Acrocanthosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, an arm, the tail, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Acrocanthosaurus atokensis was a large theropod from the Early Cretaceous of North America, roughly 125 to 100 million years ago. Its vertebrae bear unusually tall neural spines along the neck, back, and hips - researchers interpret those spines as anchoring a low muscular ridge along the dorsal line, not a tall sail like Spinosaurus. It was among the apex predators of its ecosystem in what is now the south-central United States.

J. Willis Stovall and Wann Langston named the genus in 1950. Specimen NCSM 14345 ("Fran") comes from the Antlers Formation of Oklahoma and is among the most complete skeletons known for the genus. Published length estimates center on about 11 to 11.5 m; mass estimates span about 4.4 to 8.4 tonnes across studies - this viewer uses about 6.2 t as a mid-range panel value within that range.
