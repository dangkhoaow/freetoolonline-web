TRAPPIST-1, a small, cool star just 40 light-years away, turned out to have seven planets - all roughly Earth-sized, all packed into a region smaller than Mercury's orbit around our Sun. What makes the system truly remarkable is how the planets move: each neighboring pair locks into a precise ratio of orbital periods, forming a rare chain of resonances that spans all seven worlds. Press play to watch them orbit at their real relative speeds, then toggle the habitable-zone band to see where liquid water could exist.
