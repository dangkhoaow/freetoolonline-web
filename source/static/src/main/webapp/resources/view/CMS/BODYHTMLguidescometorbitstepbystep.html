Comet Orbit 3D Explorer Step by Step

Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

The Comet Orbit 3D Explorer starts as soon as the page loads: drag to orbit the Sun and path, scroll to zoom, and press Play orbit. Everything renders on your own device - no install, no account.


Step 1 - find the ellipse

Step 1 - find the ellipse in the Comet Orbit 3D scene: look for the eccentric path with the Sun near one focus; the facts panel lists P about 76 yr, q 0.586 AU, Q 35.1 AU, and e 0.967 for the Halley-like preset.


Step 2 - scrub perihelion

Drag Orbit progress toward perihelion so tails lengthen; both cones stay roughly anti-sunward.


Step 3 - toggle tails

Step 3 - toggle tails in the Comet Orbit 3D visualizer: click Ion tail and Dust tail to hide or show each stream, then use fullscreen so controls stay visible while you orbit the view.
