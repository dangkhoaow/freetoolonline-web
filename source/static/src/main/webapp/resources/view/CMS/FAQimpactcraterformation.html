Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Impact Crater Formation 3D Explorer show?

Three real impact craters (Barringer, Chicxulub, Vredefort) rendered to a shared comparison scale, plus a test crater you grow with an impactor-diameter slider.

Why is Barringer Crater still visible next to Vredefort Dome on screen?

The scene draws crater radius on a square-root scale, so the smallest real crater (~1.19 km) stays visible next to the largest (~300 km). The facts panel always lists the real, uncompressed diameters.

How does the impactor slider decide the test crater's size?

It uses a widely cited rule of thumb for simple bowl craters: final crater diameter is roughly 20 times the impactor's diameter. This is an illustrative approximation - real crater size also depends on impact speed, angle, and target rock.

How old is the Chicxulub crater?

About 66 million years - the impact linked to the end-Cretaceous mass extinction. It is buried under the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico and was mapped through geophysical surveys and drilling.

Is Vredefort Dome really the largest impact structure on Earth?

It is among the largest confirmed impact structures, with an original diameter estimated around 300 km before roughly 2.02 billion years of erosion; today's visible dome is smaller than that original scar.

Is this a physics simulation?

No. It is an educational approximation of crater scaling, not a cratering-mechanics solver. The three real craters use published figures; the test crater uses a simple illustrative scaling rule.
