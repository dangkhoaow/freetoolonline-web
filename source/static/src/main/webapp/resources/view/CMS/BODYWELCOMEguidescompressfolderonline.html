A realistic note before you reach for the creator: ZIP shrinks text, code, JSON, CSV, and Office documents meaningfully (often 60-80% for plain text, 10-25% for DOCX or XLSX), but it barely touches already-compressed media. Photos in JPG, PNG, or HEIC typically drop 0-5%; MP4 or MOV video drops 0-3%. If the folder is photo-heavy or video-heavy and you have a 25 MB email cap to hit, compress the media first with an image compressor and then bundle the smaller copies into the .zip. Wrapping the folder in ZIP still gives you one file to share - just expect the size win to come from the media step, not from the archive step.

The optional password layer matters when the recipient is on current Windows or macOS and needs the archive to actually require a key to extract. The Settings modal on /zip-file.html offers Standard ZIP, AES-128, and AES-256; pick AES-128 or AES-256 when the password is the real privacy barrier, because Standard ZIP encryption is decades old and modern tools crack it with off-the-shelf attacks. Anonymous sessions are accepted - no account, no install, no Pro tier gate - and source files auto-delete from the build service after a short retention window, so the folder does not linger anywhere after the archive has been downloaded.
