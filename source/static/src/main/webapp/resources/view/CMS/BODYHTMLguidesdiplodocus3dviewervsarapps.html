When the Diplodocus browser viewer fits

Pick the Diplodocus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a long-tailed Late Jurassic sauropod, Size vs human at the honest about 25 m length ratio inside the disclosed 24-26 m D. carnegii range, and click facts on head, neck, body, leg, or tail - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures (152-149 Mya Kimmeridgian; Marsh 1878; Hatcher 1901 D. carnegii; CM 84 from Sheep Creek, Wyoming in 1899).


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the long silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Diplodocus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Diplodocus comparison is not

This Diplodocus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the cited D. carnegii ranges, it does not claim the tail whip-crack function is settled science, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
