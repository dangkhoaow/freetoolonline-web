How to Explore Fast Radio Bursts 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open Fast Radio Bursts 3D Explorer, pick a real burst source with the source slider, and watch a millisecond pulse travel from a distant host-galaxy marker to an Earth/CHIME receiver marker while a live waterfall canvas plots its dispersion sweep.

  1. Open /space-3d/fast-radio-bursts.html and wait for the host-galaxy marker, the Earth/CHIME receiver marker, and the waterfall canvas to render.
  2. Use the Source slider to pick Lorimer Burst (2007), FRB 121102, or FRB 180916.J0158+65.
  3. Click "Play Burst" and watch the millisecond pulse travel toward Earth, staggered by a teaching dispersion delay so higher radio frequencies arrive first.
  4. Watch the frequency-vs-time waterfall canvas plot each arriving channel as a dot, forming the classic downward dispersion-sweep signature.
  5. Read the facts panel for the selected source's duration, dispersion measure, and discovery details, plus the CHIME telescope band (400-800 MHz) and the SGR 1935+2154 Galactic-FRB link.
  6. Pick a different source on the slider and click "Play Burst" again to compare sweep speeds.
