Qianzhousaurus sinensis was a tyrannosaurid that lived in the Late Cretaceous, roughly 72-66 million years ago, in what is now southeastern China. Its most distinctive feature is a long, narrow snout studded with small horn-like bumps, making up about 70 percent of its skull length - very different from the short, deep, muscular snout of Tyrannosaurus rex. That difference earned it the nickname Pinocchio rex in early press coverage. Junchang Lu and colleagues named it in 2014 from a nearly complete skull and partial skeleton found at a construction site near Ganzhou, in the Nanxiong Formation.

The holotype individual is estimated at about 6.3 m long, 2 m tall at the hip, and 750-757 kg. Because the holotype may not have been fully grown, some researchers estimate that larger individuals could reach roughly 7.5-9 m. This page uses the holotype figures as its panel baseline and notes the wider range here because sources disagree on adult size.
