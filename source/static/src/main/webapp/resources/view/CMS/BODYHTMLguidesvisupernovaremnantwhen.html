When to Use Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

Open the Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer when you need the expanding shell after a core-collapse event - Crab Nebula teaching case ~6,500 ly, ~1,500 km/s, SN 1054 - and skip it when you need a full stellar timeline or pulsar beams alone.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer, scrub age forward from the explosion, and pause so students see the orange shell and filaments grow outward while the neutron-star core stays fixed at center.


Expansion-speed literacy in Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer

Expansion-speed literacy in Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer stays readable when you keep the facts panel open for ~6,500 ly and ~1,500 km/s while toggling filaments for a clearer wireframe view.


Sessions Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer does not fit are hydrodynamics or MHD solving, real filament-catalog mapping, or pulsar beam animation. Use Star Lifecycle 3D for the full evolutionary timeline or Neutron Star Pulsar 3D for pulsed beams. Walk the controls in the Supernova Remnant 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
