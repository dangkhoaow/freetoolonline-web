Woolly Mammoth 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

This page renders a Woolly Mammoth as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click its head, a leg, or the trunk to read a fact about that part. The Woolly Mammoth was a mammal, not a dinosaur.

Mammuthus primigenius evolved in northeastern Siberia roughly 400,000 years ago and spread across the mammoth steppe of Eurasia and North America. Adults stood up to about 3.4 m at the shoulder and weighed up to roughly 6,000 kg (about 6 tonnes), comparable to a modern African elephant, with curved tusks that could grow past 3 m long.

Most woolly mammoths died out by the end of the last Ice Age, around 10,000 years ago, but a small population survived on Wrangel Island in the Arctic Ocean until about 4,000 years ago - contemporaries of the ancient Egyptian pyramids. Frozen mummies found in Siberian permafrost, including the calf Yuka, preserve real hair, skin, and stomach contents, so mammoths are unusually well documented compared with most prehistoric animals. The species was first described as Elephas primigenius in 1799 by German naturalist Johann Friedrich Blumenbach.
