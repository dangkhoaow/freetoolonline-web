How to View Deinocheirus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Deinocheirus 3D Viewer runs a giant ornithomimosaur from Mongolia Nemegt Formation in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body region for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Deinocheirus page and take the camera

Open the Deinocheirus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. Orbit with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen keeps the controls visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean glTF may swap in from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural silhouette.


Use the four Deinocheirus controls under the canvas

Under the Deinocheirus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (11 m)

Read the Deinocheirus facts panel and click a body part

The Deinocheirus facts panel lists about 11 m panel baseline (Lee et al. 2014; Paul 2016 about 11.5 m; Molina-Perez and Larramendi 2016 about 12 m), about 3.5 m hip (panel baseline), about 5-7 t (panel baseline 6.4 t / 6,358 kg; Paul 2016 about 5 t; Molina-Perez and Larramendi 2016 about 7 t; Campione and Evans 2020 about 6.5 t), about 72-66 Mya Late Cretaceous; Nemegt Formation, Mongolia, omnivore (fish and plants in gut contents), and naming (Osmolska and Roniewicz 1970; Lee et al. 2014 resolved body plan). Huge arms about 2.4 m; sail-backed torso; beaked omnivore. Do not conflate with smaller ornithomimids such as Gallimimus. A short click - not a drag - on a body region surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. When the sourced glTF loads, the status text notes a real model with credit - CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF (seth the yutyrannus via Printables 774263) is stylized, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Deinocheirus how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not invent a single mass outside published ranges, it does not conflate Deinocheirus with smaller ornithomimids such as Gallimimus, it does not invent game score or win states, and it does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
