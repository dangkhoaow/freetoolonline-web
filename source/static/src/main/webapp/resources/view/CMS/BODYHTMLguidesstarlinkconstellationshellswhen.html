When to Use Starlink Constellation Shells 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Open Starlink Constellation Shells 3D Explorer when you need a real breakdown of Starlink's five orbital shells with published altitude, inclination, and satellite-count figures - not a live tracker.


A shell-comparison lesson

A lesson starts when you isolate each shell in turn and compare its altitude and inclination against the others, then show all five together to see how they layer around Earth.


Sessions this explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are lessons about the generic LEO/MEO/GEO altitude bands that apply to any satellite - use the Satellite Orbit Classes 3D Explorer for that - or a single spacecraft's live-style ground track; use the ISS Orbit Tracker 3D. Walk the controls in the step-by-step guide.
