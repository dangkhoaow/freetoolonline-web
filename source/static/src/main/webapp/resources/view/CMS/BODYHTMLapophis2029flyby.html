3.0 day(s) before closest approach

Published figures (NASA JPL Center for Near-Earth Object Studies + ESA NEOCC): on 13 April 2029, asteroid 99942 Apophis passes about 31,600 km from Earth's surface - closer than the geostationary satellite ring at about 35,786 km altitude. Apophis is about 340-450 m across and will be naked-eye visible over parts of the Eastern Hemisphere. Radar tracking has ruled out any impact risk for at least the next 100 years.
