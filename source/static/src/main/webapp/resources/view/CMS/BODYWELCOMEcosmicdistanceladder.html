Cosmic Distance Ladder 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Cosmic Distance Ladder 3D Explorer steps from nearby solar-system distances to stellar and nearby-galaxy scales on one continuous log zoom: 1 AU = 1.496e8 km, 1 ly = 9.461e12 km, and 1 pc = 3.086e13 km (about 3.26 ly).

Parallax is reliable roughly to about 1000 pc on the Hipparcos/Gaia teaching scale used here. Beyond that, the next classic rung is the Cepheid period-luminosity relation - named on this page as the following step, not solved as a full distance pipeline.

Use the zoom scrubber to move the yellow marker along the scale bar, Jump AU / ly / pc for presets, Play zoom for a slow auto sweep, and Ladder table to restore the teaching figures. Click Earth, rung rings, or the cursor for readouts.

  • Earth marker at the AU end of the logarithmic ladder
  • Labeled rings at 1 AU, 1 ly, 1 pc, about 1000 pc parallax reach
  • Optional about 1 Mpc nearby-galaxy hint as a label-only outer rung
  • Zoom scrub, Jump AU / ly / pc, play/pause auto-zoom
  • Facts panel with conversion table and approximation honesty
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Students compare AU, light-year, and parsec on one screen; astro readers place parallax before Cepheids; curious visitors get an honest log-scale picture instead of a fake Euclidean universe map.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

Cosmic Distance Ladder 3D Explorer is an educational log-scale visualization - it is not a true universe simulation, has no Cepheid catalog fit or SN Ia pipeline, and the about 1 Mpc ring is a label-only nearby-galaxy scale hint. For parallax geometry alone, open Parallax Distance 3D Explorer; for brightness ranking, open Stellar Magnitude 3D Explorer.
