How to Explore Algol Eclipsing Binary 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-22

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Algol Eclipsing Binary 3D Explorer, scrub the phase slider or press Play, and read the published magnitude, period, and eclipse figures as the light curve dips.

  1. Open /space-3d/algol-eclipsing-binary.html and wait for the two orbiting stars to render.
  2. Scrub the phase slider to phase 0% and watch Algol B pass in front of Algol A - the deep primary eclipse.
  3. Scrub to phase 50% and watch the much shallower secondary eclipse, when Algol A passes in front of Algol B.
  4. Press Play orbit to run one full 2.867-day orbit automatically and follow the light-curve dip below the scene.
  5. Press Top view to see why the eclipse happens; press Eclipse view to return to the observer's viewing angle.
  6. Read the facts panel for the current eclipse state plus the published period, magnitude, distance, and star figures.
