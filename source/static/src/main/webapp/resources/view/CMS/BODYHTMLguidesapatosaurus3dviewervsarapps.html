When the Apatosaurus browser viewer fits

Pick the Apatosaurus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a stocky Late Jurassic sauropod, Size vs human at the honest about 21 m length ratio inside the disclosed NHM reference and wider averages, and click facts on head, neck, body, leg, or tail - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures (152-151 Mya; Marsh 1877 A. ajax; Morrison Formation; Brontosaurus 1879 / 1903 / 2015 chronology).


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the stocky silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Apatosaurus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Apatosaurus comparison is not

This Apatosaurus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the cited ranges, it does not treat the Apatosaurus/Brontosaurus naming history as more settled than the cited 1903 / 2015 chronology, it does not invent a distinct render geometry from Diplodocus, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
