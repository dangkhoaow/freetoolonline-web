A laptop screen can look fine on the desktop yet hide defects that only a solid color fill exposes. Cycling the full-screen colors is the quickest way to spot dead or stuck pixels before a return window closes on a new machine. The readouts at the foot of the tool show your viewport size, the resolution the system reports, and the native pixel-ratio-scaled resolution - useful on high-DPI laptop panels where the physical pixel count is higher than the operating system reports.
