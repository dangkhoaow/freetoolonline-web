Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Deinocheirus?

Lee and colleagues (2014) estimated about 11 m and about 6,358 kg for the largest known specimen. Paul (2016) listed about 11.5 m and 5 t. Molina-Perez and Larramendi (2016) gave about 12 m, about 7 t, and a hip height near 4.4 m. Campione and Evans (2020) estimated about 6.5 t. This page uses 11 m and 6,358 kg as facts-panel baselines and notes the range. Turn on the human figure to compare with a 1.8 m person.

What kind of dinosaur was Deinocheirus?

It is the largest known ornithomimosaur (ostrich dinosaur) - a beaked theropod with enormous arms about 2.4 m long, a deep sail-backed torso, and a broad spatulate snout. Gut contents show fish and plant material, so it is treated as an omnivore.

When and where did Deinocheirus live?

In Late Cretaceous rocks of the Nemegt Formation in Mongolia, about 72-66 million years ago. Osmolska and Roniewicz named Deinocheirus mirificus in 1970 from giant arms; Lee and colleagues described much more complete skeletons in 2014.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited where sources disagree.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
