On 15 August 1977, the Big Ear radio telescope in Ohio picked up a strong burst of radio energy near the frequency hydrogen atoms naturally emit. It rose and fell over 72 seconds, exactly the shape expected of something fixed on the sky drifting through the telescope's beam. Astronomer Jerry Ehman circled six readings on the printout the next morning and wrote "Wow!" beside them - and despite decades of searching, it has never been seen again.
