Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Kentrosaurus?

Typical adults are commonly cited around 4-4.5 m long, with the largest known specimens reported up to about 5.5 m. Mass estimates vary widely between studies, roughly 700-1,600 kg. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a person.

How is Kentrosaurus different from Stegosaurus?

Kentrosaurus was smaller and came from a different continent - its fossils were excavated in Tanzania, while Stegosaurus fossils come from North America. Kentrosaurus also carried more spikes relative to its body length: plates run along the neck and front back, then give way to at least seven pairs of long paired spikes over the hips and tail.

When and where did Kentrosaurus live?

In the Late Jurassic, about 152 to 151 million years ago, in what is now Tanzania. Thousands of bones were excavated between 1909 and 1912 by German expeditions at the Tendaguru fossil beds. The genus was named by Edwin Hennig in 1915.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited where sources disagree.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
