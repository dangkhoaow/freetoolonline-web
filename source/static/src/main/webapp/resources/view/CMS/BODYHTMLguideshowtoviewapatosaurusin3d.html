How to View Apatosaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Apatosaurus 3D Viewer runs a stocky Late Jurassic sauropod - the NHM about 21 m / 22.4 t reference - in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the head, neck, body, a leg, or the tail for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Apatosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Apatosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit the Apatosaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene.


Use the four Apatosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Apatosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle breathing and sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest length ratio used by the viewer (about 21 m; published average range about 21-23 m, with largest known specimens near about 30 m)

Read the Apatosaurus facts panel and click body parts

The Apatosaurus facts panel lists length about 21 m (NHM reference; average about 21-23 m across sources; largest known specimens up to about 30 m), weight about 22.4 tonnes (NHM; average about 16.4-22.4 tonnes; largest known up to about 33 tonnes), the 152-151 Mya Late Jurassic window, herbivore diet, and discovery - Othniel Charles Marsh named A. ajax in 1877 from the Morrison Formation of western North America. Marsh named a second, larger 1879 skeleton Brontosaurus; the names were synonymized in 1903 with Apatosaurus keeping priority, then a 2015 diplodocid study (Tschopp et al.) reinstated Brontosaurus as a distinct genus - the page reports that chronology, not a settled final verdict. Deeper, thicker neck vertebrae support a heavier build than Diplodocus while sharing the long, low-swung neck and whip-like tail body plan. The model uses the shared sauropod builder without tallForelimbs (same branch as Diplodocus - differentiation is in published figures and naming-history copy, not a distinct mesh). A short click - not a drag - on the head, neck, body, a leg, or the tail surfaces a short fossil fact; click empty space to return to the species sheet. Skin and color are an artistic reconstruction, not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The model is procedural sauropod with no free-licensed Apatosaurus glTF re-hosted yet.


What the Apatosaurus how-to guide is not

The Apatosaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not claim soft-tissue or color accuracy, does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the cited ranges, does not treat the Apatosaurus/Brontosaurus naming history as more settled than the cited 1903 / 2015 chronology, does not invent a distinct render geometry from Diplodocus, does not invent a glTF credit, and does not invent game score or win states.
