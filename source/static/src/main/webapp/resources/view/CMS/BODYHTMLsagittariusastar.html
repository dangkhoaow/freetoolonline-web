About this scene

The facts panel fills in when the scene loads. Drag the time slider below the canvas to scrub through S2's orbit and read its published figures.

Near pericenter, S2 covers hundreds of AU in a few seconds of screen time; near apocenter, it barely seems to move - the same real physics (Kepler's second law: equal areas in equal times) that lets the closest-approach speed reach about 2.5-3% of the speed of light.

Drag to orbit and scroll or pinch to zoom. Drag the slider or press Play to move S2 along its real ellipse and watch the facts panel update.

Related orbit and gravity explorers: Black Hole 3D Explorer (the artistic render of the black hole itself), Kepler Orbits 3D Explorer (why any orbit is an ellipse in the first place), and Gravitational Redshift 3D Explorer (a relativistic effect GRAVITY also measured on S2).
