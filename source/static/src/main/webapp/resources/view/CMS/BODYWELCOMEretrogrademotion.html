Retrograde Motion 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Mars appears to loop on the sky when Earth overtakes on a faster inside orbit - the yellow line of sight sweeps, the sky trail draws the loop near opposition, and the facts panel lists Mars synodic period about 779.9 d (~26 months) with a typical retrograde arc about 72 d.

Drag to orbit the view, scroll or pinch to zoom, and press Play. Toggle Sky trail and Line of sight, jump Near opposition, and scrub Day across one synodic cycle. The Retrograde / Direct badge follows finite-difference sky elongation.

The facts panel lists synodic period about 779.9 d, typical retrograde arc about 72 d, approximate sky elongation, and whether apparent motion is direct or retrograde. Heliocentric truth stays plain: Mars keeps a direct solar orbit the whole time.

  • Sun + Earth + Mars on compressed coplanar orbits
  • Yellow Earth-to-Mars line of sight
  • Sky trail showing the apparent loop shape
  • Play / Pause, Near opposition jump, day scrub
  • Published synodic and arc figures in the facts panel
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Teachers use it to demystify retrograde as a viewpoint effect, students scrub through opposition to catch the reverse stretch, and curious readers compare the ~26-month synodic rhythm to a planet-only tour.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

The scene is an educational visualization of relative geometry - orbit sizes and trail angles are compressed. It is not a precision ephemeris, not a dated opposition calendar, not a constellation chart for tonight, and not an N-body gravity model.
