Frequently Asked Questions

Was Gorgonops a dinosaur?

No. Gorgonops was a gorgonopsian therapsid - a distant relative of mammals, not a dinosaur and not even a reptile in the strict sense paleontologists use. It lived in the Late Permian, tens of millions of years before the first dinosaurs appeared. This viewer includes it because it is a popular prehistoric predator with a distinctive pair of saber-like teeth.

How big was Gorgonops?

A 2023 study of a near-complete skeleton estimated Gorgonops torvus at roughly 1.5 to 2 m nose to tail and about 98 kg, close to the mass of a modern lioness. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

When and where did Gorgonops live?

About 260 to 254 million years ago, in the Late Permian, in what is now the Karoo Basin of South Africa. It lived tens of millions of years before the first dinosaurs evolved.

What made Gorgonops distinctive?

A pair of saber-like upper canine teeth, among the largest of any gorgonopsian relative to its skull size, used to kill prey. It was a carnivore and one of the apex predators of its Permian ecosystem, though later gorgonopsians such as Inostrancevia grew considerably larger.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body proportions follow published skeletal studies, but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values.
