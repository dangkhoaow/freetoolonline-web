When the Gallimimus browser viewer fits

Pick the Gallimimus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a fast, toothless, ostrich-like runner, Size vs human at the honest 6 m length ratio inside the disclosed about 6 m (up to 7-8 m) range, and click facts on the neck, head, arm, tail, or leg - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures (~70 Mya; Osmolska, Roniewicz and Barsbold 1972; Thulborn 1982 speed) and the unsettled diet and feathering claims.


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Gallimimus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Gallimimus comparison is not

This Gallimimus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length, weight, or speed beyond the cited ranges, it does not invent a settled diet or direct Gallimimus feather fossils, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
