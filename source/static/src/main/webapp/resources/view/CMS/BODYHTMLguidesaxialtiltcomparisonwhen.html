When to Use Axial Tilt Comparison 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Open the Axial Tilt Comparison 3D Explorer when you need a side-by-side tilt comparison across all eight planets - without a climate or orbital-mechanics solver.


A five-minute seasons lesson

A five-minute seasons lesson starts when you open the explorer, click Earth and Mars in turn to show their similar tilts, then click Jupiter to show why a near-upright planet has almost no seasons.


Explaining Uranus and Venus in one glance

Explaining Uranus and Venus in one glance works by clicking each in turn - Uranus for the sideways 97.77-degree tilt from an ancient collision, Venus for the retrograde-rotation convention behind its 177.4-degree figure.


Sessions Axial Tilt Comparison 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are real relative planet sizes, orbital-period comparisons, or precession modeling. Use Planet Size Comparison 3D Explorer for real sizes, or Uranus Tilt 3D Explorer for one planet in close-up. Walk the controls in the Axial Tilt Comparison 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
