How the Tarbosaurus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Tarbosaurus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 10.5 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Tarbosaurus

What a person would see is a large Asian tyrannosaurid whose panel length is about 10.5 m - nearly six person-lengths end to end - with standing height about 3.5 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how this close Tyrannosaurus relative compares to you.


What the Tarbosaurus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays in the published about 4-5 t range with only the panel baseline 4.5 t disclosed - the viewer does not claim one mass outside that range. Soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. Tarbosaurus is a distinct species from Tyrannosaurus, not the same animal.
