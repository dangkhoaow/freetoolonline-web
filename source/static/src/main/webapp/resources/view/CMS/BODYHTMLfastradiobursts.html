About this scene

The facts panel fills when the scene loads. Pick a source and press Play Burst.

A fast radio burst is a millisecond-scale flash of radio waves from far outside the Milky Way. The Lorimer Burst (2007) carried a dispersion measure of 375 pc/cm^3, the signature astronomers read as a distance ruler through intergalactic plasma. FRB 121102 was the first confirmed to repeat; FRB 180916.J0158+65 repeats on a measured 16.35-day cycle, discovered by the CHIME telescope in Canada.
