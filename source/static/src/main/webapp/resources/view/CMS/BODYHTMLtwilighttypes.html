About this scene

The facts panel fills when the scene loads. Move the depression dial to see which twilight band it points to.

The globe looks straight down the Sun-Earth line, so daylight is the bright cap at the top and full night is the dark cap at the bottom - this is not the usual side-on Earth view, it is chosen so all three twilight bands are visible at once as complete rings.

Drag to orbit the scene and scroll or pinch to zoom. The bright thin ring marks the exact depression angle set by the dial; the small glowing marker shows the latitude and season point you picked.
