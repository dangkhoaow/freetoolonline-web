When to Use Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

The Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer fits classroom demos of apparent vs absolute magnitude, short curiosity breaks on the Pogson 2.512x step, and literacy around Sun, Sirius, Vega, and the naked-eye limit. It is educational brightness geometry - not a star catalog - so a few session types need another tool.


A five-minute classroom demo

Open Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer, toggle Apparent then Absolute mag, and watch the Sun shrink at 10 pc - clearer than a crowded lifecycle scrub alone.


Numbers in one panel

Keep the facts table visible while reading Sun about -26.74, Sirius about -1.46, Vega about 0.03, and the about 2.512x flux step.


Sessions it does not fit

These sessions do not fit Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer: any time you need evolutionary stages, galactic arms, or planet tours. Use Star Lifecycle 3D Explorer, Milky Way Map 3D Explorer, or Solar System 3D Explorer instead. It also needs WebGL in the browser.
