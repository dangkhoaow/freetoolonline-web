Como ver Abelisaurus en 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Revisado por el equipo editorial de FreetoolOnline

Abelisaurus 3D Viewer runs a skull-only Argentine abelisaurid theropod in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the skull, a short forelimb, or a hind leg for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Abelisaurus page and take the camera

Open the Abelisaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. Orbit with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen keeps the controls visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean glTF may swap in from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural silhouette.


Use the four Abelisaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Abelisaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (7.4 m)

Read the Abelisaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Abelisaurus facts panel lists length about 7.2-11 m (panel baseline 7.4 m), weight about 1.4-3 t (panel baseline 2 t), height about 2.5 m, era 83-80 Mya Late Cretaceous Anacleto Formation Argentina, carnivore diet, named by Bonaparte and Novas in 1985. Abelisaurus is known from a partial skull only - the full body follows related abelisaurids, not a complete Abelisaurus skeleton. Carnotaurus and Majungasaurus are abelisaurid siblings on this site for comparison - not the same animal. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. Optional CC0/PD glTF (Model Magic via Printables 1767713) may swap in. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Abelisaurus how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not claim a complete Abelisaurus skeleton is known, it does not treat Carnotaurus or Majungasaurus as identical to Abelisaurus, and it does not invent game score or win states.
