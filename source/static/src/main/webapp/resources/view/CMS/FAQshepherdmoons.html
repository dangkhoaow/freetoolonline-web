Frequently Asked Questions

What is a shepherd moon?

A small moon orbiting just inside or outside a ring whose gravity herds the ring particles, keeping a narrow ring thin and sharp-edged or clearing a gap.

How do two shepherds confine a ring?

The inner moon orbits faster and tugs inward-drifting particles back out; the outer moon orbits slower and pulls outward-drifting particles back in. Between them the particles stay penned.

Which moons shepherd Saturn F ring?

Prometheus on the inside and Pandora on the outside, found by Voyager 1 in 1980. In reality the F ring is chaotic and dominated by Prometheus, which also raises its kinks and streamers.

Which moons shepherd Uranus epsilon ring?

Cordelia at about 49,752 km on the inside and Ophelia at about 53,764 km on the outside, found by Voyager 2 in 1986. This is the clean textbook example of shepherding.

Who came up with the idea?

Peter Goldreich and Scott Tremaine proposed shepherd moons in 1979 to explain why Uranus rings were so narrow, before the moons themselves had been seen.

Is this scene to scale?

No. The confinement is illustrative rather than a gravity solver, the sizes and distances are not to scale, and the motion is sped up so the spreading and herding show in seconds.
