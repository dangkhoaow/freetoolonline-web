How to View Titanosaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Titanosaurus 3D Viewer runs a Late Cretaceous Indian titanosaur - length about 9-12 m (panel 10.5 m), mass about 4-13 t (panel 5.5 t). Drag, zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person, and click a body region.


Open the Titanosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Titanosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Titanosaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural sauropod silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Titanosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Titanosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio used by the viewer (10.5 m within the about 9-12 m published range)

Read the Titanosaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Titanosaurus facts panel lists length about 9-12 m (panel baseline 10.5 m), hip height about 3-3.5 m (panel 3.2 m), mass about 4-13 t (panel baseline 5.5 t / 5500 kg), the Late Cretaceous window about 70-66 million years ago in the Lameta Formation of India, herbivore diet, and naming from Lydekker 1877 - often treated as a nomen dubium, and historically the first titanosaur named. A short click - not a drag - on a body region surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. When the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Titanosaurus how-to guide is not

The Titanosaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not invent a single mass outside the published 4-13 t range, does not claim the genus is uncontested, does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, does not invent game score or win states, and does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
