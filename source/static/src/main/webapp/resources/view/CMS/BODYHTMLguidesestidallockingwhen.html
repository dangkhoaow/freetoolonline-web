When to Use Tidal Locking 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

Open the Tidal Locking 3D Explorer when you need synchronous Moon spin matching the 27.32-day sidereal orbit - with recession 3.8 cm/yr and day lengthening 1.7 ms/century in the panel - without a tidal-friction physics solver.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Tidal Locking 3D Explorer in Tidally locked mode, press Play orbit, then switch Unlocked spin so students see the far side appear - faster than a chalkboard sketch alone.


Recession literacy in Tidal Locking 3D Explorer

Recession and day-lengthening literacy in Tidal Locking 3D Explorer stays readable when you keep the facts panel open for 3.8 cm/yr and 1.7 ms/century while the near-side marker stays Earthward in locked mode.


Sessions Tidal Locking 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Tidal Locking 3D Explorer does not fit are Moon phase lit fraction, lunar libration as the main lesson, or live ephemerides. Use Moon Phases 3D Explorer or Moon Calendar 3D Explorer instead. Walk the controls in the Tidal Locking 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
