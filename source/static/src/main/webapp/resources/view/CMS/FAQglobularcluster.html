Frequently Asked Questions

What is a globular star swarm?

A dense, roughly spherical swarm of old stars. Milky Way examples number about 150 known (up to about 200 estimated), with ages about 11-13 billion years and diameters about 50-450 light-years.

How many stars do they hold?

Published counts run from about 10,000 to ~10 million. Omega Centauri sits near the top end at about 10 million stars.

How is this different from the Milky Way map page?

The Milky Way map teaches galaxy-wide structure. This page teaches one star swarm's packed core, halo extent, census, age, and size figures.

How is this different from the constellation sphere?

Constellation sphere teaches stick-figure sky lore. This page teaches a real star-star swarm population and its published scale figures.

Are the on-screen star counts exact?

No. The canvas draws a teaching sample. The scrubber maps onto the published 10,000 to ~10 million range shown in the facts panel.

Does this page run N-body stellar dynamics?

No. It is an educational explorer using published NASA/ESA catalog-scale figures - not an N-body stellar dynamics model.
