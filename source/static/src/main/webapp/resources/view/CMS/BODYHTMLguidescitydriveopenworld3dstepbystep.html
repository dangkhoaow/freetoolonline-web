How to Play City Drive Open World 3D - Step by Step

Last reviewed: 2026-07-06

City Drive Open World 3D starts in free-roam: your car sits on a city street, and the HUD with the Start button renders before the 3D engine even finishes loading. Here is each step from first throttle to a saved best run.


Step 1 - drive

W or the up arrow accelerates, S or the down arrow brakes and then reverses, A / D or the left / right arrows steer. On a phone, hold the on-screen Left / Gas / Brake / Right pedals. Steering strength scales with speed and the car leans into turns; a chase camera follows from behind.


Step 2 - learn the streets

The city is a 5x5 grid of blocks on a road lattice. Other cars drive the lanes in both directions - hitting one, or a building, pushes you out with a soft bounce and bleeds your speed. That is the whole penalty: there is no damage model.


Step 3 - start a checkpoint run

Click Start checkpoint run. You get 60 seconds; a glowing ring waits at a distant intersection and the arrow above your car points straight at it. Drive through the ring: plus one to your score, plus 8 seconds to the clock, and the next ring appears somewhere else.


Step 4 - when time runs out

The run ends, your score is compared with your record - your record run is saved in this browser's local storage - and free roam continues. Restart run starts a fresh 60 seconds whenever you want. The game pauses itself while the tab is hidden.


Step 5 - sound and fullscreen

Engine hum and checkpoint chimes are synthesized on your device and stay off until you click Sound. The Fullscreen button puts the whole scene on your screen with the HUD still visible; Escape exits.
