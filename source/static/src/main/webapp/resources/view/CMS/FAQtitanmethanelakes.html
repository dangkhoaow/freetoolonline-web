Frequently Asked Questions

What is Titan?

Saturn's largest moon - the only other body in the solar system with stable liquid on its surface (methane and ethane lakes and seas) and a thick nitrogen-methane atmosphere (NASA Cassini).

How thick is Titan's atmosphere?

About 95% nitrogen and 5% methane, detectable to roughly 600 km altitude - about ten times taller than Earth's - with surface pressure about 1.45 times Earth's sea level.

What did Cassini's radar find?

Liquid methane and ethane lakes and seas concentrated near the poles, covering only a few percent of the surface, including Kraken Mare (largest, roughly 400,000 sq km) and Ligeia Mare (depth measured up to about 160 m).

Is there an ocean under the ice?

Cassini gravity data and Huygens radio signals point to a subsurface liquid-water ocean roughly 55-80 km beneath the ice shell.

What was the Huygens landing?

ESA's Huygens probe landed on Titan on 2005-01-14 - the most distant landing by a human-made craft - and transmitted data for over 90 minutes from the surface.

Is this a physical simulation?

No. It is an educational explorer using published NASA, Cassini, and Huygens figures - haze, layers, and lake positions are schematic teaching cues, not a physics model.
