How to View Mosasaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Mosasaurus 3D Viewer runs a reconstructive marine reptile in your browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the head, a flipper, or the tail to read a part fact.


Open the Mosasaurus page and take the camera

Open the Mosasaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit the Mosasaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene.


Use the four Mosasaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Mosasaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation - freezes auto-spin (the label flips to Auto-rotate when spinning is off)
  • Idle motion - adds a subtle flipper sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person scaled to the catalogue ~17 m length (published range about 11-17 m) so the comparison stays honest

Read the Mosasaurus facts panel and click body parts

The Mosasaurus facts panel lists length (about 11-17 m), weight (up to about 10 tonnes), the 82-66 Mya Late Cretaceous window, diet (carnivore), and that it is a marine reptile - not a dinosaur. A short click - not a drag - on the head, a flipper, or the tail swaps in a part note; click empty space to return to the species sheet. Skin color is an artistic reconstruction; the figures are the published ones.


What the Mosasaurus how-to guide is not

The Mosasaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR) and it does not claim soft-tissue accuracy or that Mosasaurus walked on land.
