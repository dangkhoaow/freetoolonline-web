How the Gryponyx size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Gryponyx model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 5 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Gryponyx

What a person would see is a hooked-claw Early Jurassic sauropodomorph whose panel length is about 5 m - nearly three body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 1.8 m high in the panel baseline. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the snout to feel how an Upper Elliot Formation herbivore compares to you.


What the Gryponyx size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays poorly constrained with only one secondary estimate about 485 kg disclosed - the viewer does not claim mass is tightly constrained from fossils. Soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The Massospondylus synonymy history is noted in the facts panel but does not change these published size figures. For the control walkthrough see how to view Gryponyx in 3D.
