When to Play City Drive Open World 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-06

City Drive Open World 3D gets you driving around a 3D city block grid in your browser with nothing installed - a 0.7 MB engine loads once, then stays cached. Cruise freely with no objective at all, or start a timed checkpoint run when you feel like a game.


When it fits

Pick the browser version when the point is driving right now: a short break, a shared computer, or a phone - the same page serves keyboard (W/S/A/D and the arrows) and touch (on-screen Left / Gas / Brake / Right pedals). It also fits when you want RELAXED driving: bumping a building or a passing car just slows you down with a soft bounce, so there is no damage, no fail state, and nothing to harm - you can drive as badly as you like.


What to expect

A 5x5 block grid of procedural buildings with a road lattice between them, other cars driving the lanes in both directions, and a chase camera that follows your car with a lean into the turns. Free roam is endless. The checkpoint run starts a 60-second clock: an arrow over your car points to a glowing ring at an intersection, and every ring you reach scores one and adds 8 seconds - when the clock runs out, your best run is saved in this browser's local storage. Engine hum and checkpoint chimes are synthesized on your device and stay muted until you click the sound toggle.

For the controls and the run rules in detail, see City Drive step by step.
