The outlined cell is the picked case's real assigned rating.

The Torino Impact Hazard Scale turns two things - impact probability and expected consequence - into one 0-10 number the public can read at a glance. 99942 Apophis carries the highest rating ever assigned to a real object (Torino 4, December 2004) before a longer observed arc brought it back down to 0 in 2006.

Drag to orbit and scroll or pinch to zoom. For the single 2013 impact event that happened with no advance warning at all, see the Chelyabinsk Meteor 3D Explorer; for the geometry of Apophis's one specific 2029 close approach, see the Apophis 2029 Flyby 3D Explorer.
