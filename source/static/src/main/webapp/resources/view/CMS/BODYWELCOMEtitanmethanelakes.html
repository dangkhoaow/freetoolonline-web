Titan Methane Lakes 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-22

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

This browser explorer shows Saturn's moon Titan as a hazy nitrogen-methane world with polar methane and ethane seas Cassini's radar mapped beneath the haze, plus a cutaway of the ice shell and the subsurface liquid-water ocean.

Titan's atmosphere is about 95% nitrogen and 5% methane, detectable to roughly 600 km altitude - about ten times taller than Earth's. Surface pressure is about 1.45 times Earth's sea-level pressure and surface temperature is about -179 C (94 K). Cassini radar found liquid methane and ethane lakes and seas concentrated near the poles, covering only a few percent of the surface, fed by a complete methane hydrological cycle of evaporation, hydrocarbon clouds, rain, and surface flow.

  • Toggle the haze to reveal the radar-mapped polar seas
  • Toggle the cutaway to see the ice shell and subsurface ocean
  • Read atmosphere, pressure, temperature, and sea-size figures
  • Compare with the Enceladus Geysers sibling (plumes, not lakes)
  • Drag to orbit, scroll or pinch to zoom
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached.

This is an educational approximation - haze thickness, layer sizes, and lake positions are exaggerated and schematic for visibility, not a precise radar map.
