Where Satellite Orbit Classes wins

Satellite Orbit Classes wins when the question is "why do different satellites live at different altitudes" - all five bands animate together at their real relative speeds, so a GPS satellite's MEO and a weather satellite's GEO are directly comparable in one view.


Where ISS Orbit Tracker wins

ISS Orbit Tracker wins when the question is about one real spacecraft in detail - it plots a yellow ground track under the station's path and lets you scrub the orbit phase, which a five-band comparison scene does not attempt.


A reasonable rule

As a reasonable rule, use Satellite Orbit Classes to compare orbit families and pick the right band for a mission profile; use ISS Orbit Tracker to study one spacecraft's ground track in detail; use Hohmann Transfer 3D Explorer for the maneuver that moves a satellite between two bands. The published figures in each panel stay accurate either way.
