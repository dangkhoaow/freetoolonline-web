When the Carnotaurus browser viewer fits

Pick the Carnotaurus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a horned Late Cretaceous abelisaurid, Size vs human at the honest about 7.8 m length ratio inside the disclosed 7.5-8 m core and wider estimates, and click facts on head, arm, tail, or leg - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures (69-66 Mya; Bonaparte 1985; La Colonia Formation).


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the horned silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Carnotaurus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Carnotaurus comparison is not

This Carnotaurus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the cited ranges, it does not claim the tail and lower legs are known from the holotype, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
