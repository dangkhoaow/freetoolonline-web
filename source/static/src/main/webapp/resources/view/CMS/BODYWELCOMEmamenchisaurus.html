This page renders a Mamenchisaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, neck, body, a leg, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Mamenchisaurus lived mainly in the Late Jurassic of China (Sichuan Basin and Yunnan), about 160 to 145 million years ago for the more commonly cited species. Named by C.C. Young (Yang Zhongjian) in 1954, the genus is famous for an extreme neck. This page uses M. hochuanensis as the baseline body (about 18 m long, about 11.8 tonnes, neck longer than 9 m) and treats the longer M. sinocanadorum neck claim as a separate, fragmentary-fossil fact.
