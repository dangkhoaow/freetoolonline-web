Tidal Heating 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

This browser explorer shows tidal heating - what the Io:Europa:Ganymede 1:2:4 Laplace resonance actually does to two moons. Io flexes constantly and hosts an estimated ~400 active volcanoes (NASA Juno); Europa keeps a global subsurface liquid-water ocean beneath a relatively thin ice shell.

Orbital Resonance 3D owns the period-ratio mechanics of the 1:2:4 chain itself. Galilean Moons 3D owns the four-moon overview. This page owns the thermal and geological consequence of that resonance.

  • Io flexing with flickering orange volcanic hotspots
  • An Europa ocean-cutaway toggle that reveals the blue subsurface ocean under its icy shell
  • Ganymede completing the 1:2:4 orbital chain for context
  • Facts panel lists Io volcano count, surface flex, heat flow, and the 2025 Juno interior finding
  • Distinct from orbital-resonance and galilean-moons
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Students see why Io is the most volcanic body known; teachers connect resonance mechanics to a real geological consequence; curious readers learn Europa ocean is a leading target in the search for habitability.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

This is an educational schematic - not an interior-structure or magma-flow solver. Io flexing and hotspot flicker are stylized for visibility; the figures in the table are real.
