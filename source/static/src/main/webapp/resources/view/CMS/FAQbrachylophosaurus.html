Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Brachylophosaurus?

Adults reached at least about 9 m long. Published figures range from the Natural History Museum directory's 7.0 m up to Gregory S. Paul's 2010 maximum near 11 m and about 7 tonnes; other mass estimates fall around 4-5 tonnes. This page uses 9 m and 5500 kg as the facts-panel baselines. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

What does the short crest mean?

The genus name means short-crested lizard. Unlike the long tubular crest of Parasaurolophus, Brachylophosaurus had a low, flat, paddle-like crest of bone on top of the skull. Soft-tissue color around the crest is not known from fossils.

When and where did Brachylophosaurus live?

In the Late Cretaceous Campanian, about 81 to 76 million years ago, in floodplain deposits of the Oldman Formation in Alberta and the Judith River Formation in Montana. Charles M. Sternberg named Brachylophosaurus canadensis in 1953.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited where sources disagree.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
