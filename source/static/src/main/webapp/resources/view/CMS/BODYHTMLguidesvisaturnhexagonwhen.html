When to Use Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

Open the Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer when you need the polar hexagon jet - ~30,000 km across, ~120 m/s - and skip it when you need ring-band radii or belt banding alone.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer, scrub rotation phase, and pause so students see the jet particles keep tracing the same six-sided outline while the globe spins beneath them.


Polar-jet literacy in Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer

Polar-jet literacy in Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer stays readable when you keep the facts panel open for ~30,000 km / ~120 m/s and hide the rings for a clearer pole view.


Sessions Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer does not fit are fluid-dynamics modeling, Cassini camera-frame mapping, or ring-band radius study. Use Saturn Rings 3D for band radii or Gas Giant Atmosphere 3D for belt patterns. Walk the controls in the Saturn Hexagon 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
