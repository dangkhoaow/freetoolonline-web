A young star is not born alone - it forms inside a flattened, spinning disk of gas and dust. In 2014 the ALMA telescope array imaged one of these protoplanetary disks around HL Tau in unprecedented detail, revealing concentric bright rings separated by dark gaps. This explorer places the real ALMA-measured ring and gap distances around a model star. Press play to watch the disk turn, and toggle the gaps to see where astronomers think young planets are clearing their orbits.
