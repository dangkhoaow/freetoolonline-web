Globular Star Swarm 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

This browser explorer shows a globular star swarm as a dense, ancient star swarm with a packed core and wider halo, using published census, age, and size figures in the facts panel.

About 150 Milky Way globular star swarms are known (up to about 200 estimated). Ages are about 11-13 billion years. Diameters span roughly 50-450 light-years. Star counts run from about 10,000 to ~10 million (Omega Centauri near ~10 million).

  • Scrub teaching star count across the published range
  • Scrub diameter from about 50 to 450 light-years
  • Read census, age, diameter, and star-count figures
  • Compare with Milky Way map and constellation sphere siblings
  • Drag to orbit, scroll or pinch to zoom
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

For galaxy structure, open the Milky Way Map 3D Explorer. For stick-figure constellations, open the Constellation Sphere 3D Explorer.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached.

This is an educational approximation - on-screen star count is a teaching sample mapped to the published range. It is not an N-body stellar dynamics model.
