When to Use Neutron Star Pulsar 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

The Neutron Star Pulsar 3D Explorer fits classroom demos of the lighthouse pulse, short curiosity breaks on 12 km / 1.4 Msun, and literacy around beaming versus horizons. It is an educational geometry sketch - not a timing catalog - so a few session types need another tool.


A five-minute classroom demo

Open Neutron Star Pulsar 3D Explorer, press Play spin, then Beam so students see why a tilted magnet makes pulses on Earth.


Numbers in one panel

Keep the facts table visible while reading radius about 12 km, mass about 1.4 Msun, and fastest spins near 716 Hz.


Sessions it does not fit

These sessions do not fit Neutron Star Pulsar 3D Explorer: any time you need an event horizon, a full star-life path, or planet orbits. Use Black Hole 3D Explorer, Star Lifecycle 3D Explorer, or Solar System 3D Explorer instead. It also needs WebGL in the browser.
