Deinocheirus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

This page renders a Deinocheirus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, a limb, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Deinocheirus lived in the Late Cretaceous of Mongolia (Nemegt Formation), about 72-66 million years ago. Osmolska and Roniewicz named Deinocheirus mirificus in 1970 from giant arms; Lee and colleagues described much more complete skeletons in 2014. It is the largest known ornithomimosaur, with a sail-backed torso and arms about 2.4 m long.
