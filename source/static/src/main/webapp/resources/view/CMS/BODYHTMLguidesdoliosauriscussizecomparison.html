How the Doliosauriscus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Doliosauriscus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 3.5 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Doliosauriscus

What a person would see is a stocky anteosaurid synapsid whose panel length is about 3.5 m - almost twice a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 1.4 m high and a skull about 53 cm long. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the head to feel how far this mammal-line predator sits from any dinosaur silhouette.


What the Doliosauriscus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a peer-reviewed consensus mass census: the about 500 kg panel figure is a proportionate reconstruction estimate, not a tightly published primary value.
