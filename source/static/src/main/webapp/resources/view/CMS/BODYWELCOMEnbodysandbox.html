N-Body Sandbox 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

This browser explorer is a real N-body gravity sandbox: click anywhere to add a body, and every pair of bodies pulls on every other pair with F = G m1 m2 / r^2. The real Newtonian constant of gravitation, G = 6.674 30 x 10^-11 m^3 kg^-1 s^-2 (CODATA 2022), is shown for literacy alongside a scaled teaching constant that keeps motion visible on one screen.

Three-Body Problem 3D owns a fixed classic figure-8 choreography and explicitly discloses it is not a general N-body ephemeris. This page fills exactly that gap: a free-form sandbox where you set the initial conditions and watch bound orbits, scatters, and captures emerge.

  • Click the ground to add a body with a small randomized velocity
  • Light or heavy mass presets for the next body you add
  • Fading orbit trails per body
  • Facts panel lists the real G value and a live bound-versus-escaping count
  • Distinct from three-body-problem (fixed choreography) and gravity-well (static potential surfaces)
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Students see emergent orbits, scatters, and captures from the same simple force law; teachers use it to contrast a scripted choreography with genuine emergent dynamics; curious readers connect Newton real G to a hands-on sandbox.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

This is an educational N-body sandbox using a scaled gravitational constant for visible motion in one screen - the real G value above is shown for literacy. Gravity is softened at close range to avoid a numerical blow-up, standard N-body practice, and this is not a general-purpose ephemeris.
