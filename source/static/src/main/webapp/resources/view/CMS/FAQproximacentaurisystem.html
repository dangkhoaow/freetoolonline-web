Frequently Asked Questions

How far away is Proxima Centauri?

4.2465 light-years - the closest known star to the Sun.

Does Proxima Centauri have a habitable planet?

Proxima b, discovered in 2016, orbits inside the star's habitable zone every 11.2 days. Whether it is actually habitable is a separate, open question because Proxima Centauri is an active flare star.

How many planets does Proxima Centauri have?

Two confirmed (Proxima b and Proxima d) and one unconfirmed candidate (Proxima c).

What is Proxima d?

A sub-Earth planet, about 0.26 Earth masses, confirmed in 2022 on a tight 5.12-day orbit - too close to the star to be habitable.

What is Proxima c?

An unconfirmed candidate roughly 7 Earth masses on a distant, roughly 5.28-year orbit - too cold to be habitable even if confirmed.

Why does a flare matter for habitability?

Proxima Centauri is a known flare star. Its own 2016 discovery team flagged stellar flares as an open question for whether any of its planets could keep an atmosphere, which is why this scene includes a flare button next to the habitable-zone band.

Is this scene to scale?

No. Orbit distances use a compressed, non-linear scale so the close-in planets and the far candidate all fit in one view, and the star and planets are enlarged for visibility. The real AU figures are in the facts table.
