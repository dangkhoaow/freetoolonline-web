Frequently Asked Questions

Was Tylosaurus a dinosaur?

No. Tylosaurus was a mosasaur - an ocean-going lizard - that lived in the Late Cretaceous seas. This viewer includes it because it is a popular prehistoric marine predator, not because it was a dinosaur.

How big was Tylosaurus?

Large adults are often cited near 8-14 m, with some specimens estimated around 12-13.2 m. Mass estimates range from about 1.1 tonnes for mid-size adults to several tonnes for the largest forms. Turn on the human figure to judge the scale.

How is Tylosaurus different from Mosasaurus?

Tylosaurus is best known from the North American Western Interior Seaway and has a long toothless tip to the snout. Mosasaurus is the European Maastricht form often reconstructed longer near 11-17 m. Both were mosasaurs, not dinosaurs.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body shape follows fossil reconstructions - streamlined trunk, four flippers, a finned tail - but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction. Fossils preserve bone, not color. The figures shown are real published values with ranges where sources disagree.

When and where did Tylosaurus live?

About 90-66 million years ago in Late Cretaceous seas, especially the Western Interior Seaway that covered much of what is now the central United States and Canada.
