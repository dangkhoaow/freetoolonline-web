Avaceratops 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

This page renders an Avaceratops as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the frill, a horn, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Avaceratops lived in the Late Cretaceous, about 77 million years ago, in the Judith River Formation of Montana. Dodson named A. lammersi in 1986. It is among the smallest known ceratopsids. The holotype is about 2.3 m (maturity debated); a referred skull (MOR 692) implies about 4.2 m for a larger individual, with mass about 1 tonne for a 4 m animal (Paul 2010).
