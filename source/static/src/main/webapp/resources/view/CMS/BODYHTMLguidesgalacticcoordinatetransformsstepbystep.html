How to Explore Galactic Coordinate Transforms 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Galactic Coordinate Transforms 3D Explorer, scrub the precession-year slider, and watch the equatorial plane trace its real 25,772-year path around the fixed ecliptic plane.

  1. Open /space-3d/galactic-coordinate-transforms.html and wait for the three tilted planes and starfield to render.
  2. Scrub the precession-year slider away from "Today" and watch the red equatorial plane rotate around the fixed yellow ecliptic plane.
  3. Press "Play precession" to run the cycle automatically at an accelerated rate.
  4. Click "Thuban (2700 BCE)" to jump to the era when Thuban was the pole star, then "Vega (14,000 CE)" to see the opposite side of the circle.
  5. Toggle the ecliptic, galactic, or equatorial plane checkboxes off one at a time to see the remaining planes more clearly.
  6. Read the facts panel for the live precession-clock year, the nearest pole star, and the published angle and period figures.
