How to View Cryolophosaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Cryolophosaurus 3D Viewer runs an Early Jurassic Antarctic theropod with a forward-facing crest - holotype length about 6-7 m (Size vs human uses 6.5 m), mass about 350-465 kg commonly cited. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body part for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Cryolophosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Cryolophosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Cryolophosaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural theropod with zero regression.


Use the four Cryolophosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Cryolophosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle breathing and leg sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest length ratio used by the viewer (6.5 m representative within the published about 6-7 m holotype range; some estimates about 7.7-8 m)

Read the Cryolophosaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Cryolophosaurus facts panel lists holotype length about 6-7 m (some estimates about 7.7 m from Molina-Perez and Larramendi 2016; NHM directory about 8.0 m; Size vs human uses 6.5 m), hip height about 1.8-2.2 m for a large adult, mass about 350-465 kg commonly cited for the holotype (some estimates about 780 kg; NHM about 500 kg - cite the range), the Early Jurassic window about 194-188 million years ago in Antarctica (Hanson Formation; NHM lists about 170 Mya and differs), carnivore diet, and naming by Hammer and Hickerson in 1994 (type species C. ellioti). Distinct from Dilophosaurus (paired crests, North America). A short click - not a drag - on head, body, leg, or tail surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction; when the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The Elvisaurus nickname is historical popular usage only - this guide does not claim the crest looked exactly like Elvis hair.


What the Cryolophosaurus how-to guide is not

The Cryolophosaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the disclosed about 6-7 m / 350-465 kg ranges (with larger estimates disclosed), does not claim the crest looked exactly like Elvis hair, does not claim soft-tissue color accuracy, does not invent game score or win states, and does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
