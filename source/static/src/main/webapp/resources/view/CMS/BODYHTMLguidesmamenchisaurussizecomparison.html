How the Mamenchisaurus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Mamenchisaurus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 18 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Mamenchisaurus

What a person would see is an extreme-neck sauropod whose panel length is about 18 m - ten person-lengths end to end - with a neck over 9 m alone and standing height about 5.5 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how half the body being neck compares to you.


What the Mamenchisaurus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a blended composite: the M. hochuanensis headline figures (18 m / 11.8 t / 9+ m neck) are never merged with the M. sinocanadorum record-neck figure (about 15 m from fragmentary bones only) or with extrapolated genus-wide 22-35 m / 50-75 t extremes. M. youngi (about 16 m / 6.5 m neck) stays corroborating and separate. Soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
