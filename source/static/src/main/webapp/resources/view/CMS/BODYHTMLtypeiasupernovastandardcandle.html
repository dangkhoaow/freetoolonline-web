About this scene

The facts panel fills in when the scene loads. Scrub the sequence slider through accretion and explosion, and the distance slider to see the distance-modulus math update.

Drag the sequence slider from 0 to 70 to watch the white dwarf's mass climb toward the Chandrasekhar limit as it pulls matter from the red-giant donor; past 70 the dwarf explodes and the panel switches to a fading light-curve readout. Press Play sequence to run the whole thing automatically.

The distance slider does not change the scene's geometry - it changes the apparent magnitude in the facts panel, using the same distance-modulus formula astronomers use when a Type Ia supernova's near-uniform peak brightness lets them read off a distance from how faint it looks.
