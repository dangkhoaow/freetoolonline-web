Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Pluto-Charon called a double planet?

The shared barycenter sits outside Pluto (~2,110 km from Pluto's center vs a 1188.3 km mean radius), and both bodies are tidally locked with a 6.387-day synchronous period. That exterior barycenter plus mutual lock is the classic near double-planet geometry.

How is this different from the Earth-Moon barycenter page?

In the Earth-Moon system the barycenter sits inside Earth. Here the barycenter is outside Pluto. Open the Earth-Moon Barycenter 3D Explorer for the ordinary planet-moon case.

How is this different from dwarf-planet comparison?

Dwarf-planet comparison teaches relative sizes in a lineup. This page teaches mutual orbit, tidal lock, and an outside-primary barycenter. Open the Dwarf Planet Comparison 3D Explorer for the size lineup.

Are the on-screen distances to scale?

No. Distances are compressed so both worlds and the barycenter stay readable. Radii, masses, separation, and barycenter location still use published NASA New Horizons / JPL figures.

Does this page integrate gravity?

No. It is an educational explorer using published figures for circular mutual orbits. It is not a gravity integrator and does not solve N-body equations.

What figures does the facts panel use?

Pluto 1188.3 km, Charon 606 km, masses 1.303e22 kg / 1.586e21 kg (~8.2:1), separation ~19,591 km, barycenter ~2,110 km from Pluto center, period 6.387 days, both tidally locked (NASA New Horizons / JPL).
