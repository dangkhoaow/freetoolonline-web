Sun Structure 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

A schematic cutaway of the Sun highlights core, radiative zone, convective zone, photosphere, and corona - teaching layers and temperatures, not a hydrodynamics visualization.

The core reaches about 15.7 million K where fusion occurs. The radiative zone moves energy outward by radiation. The convective zone is boiling plasma. The photosphere at about 5,772 K is the visible surface. The corona at about 1-3 million K is the outer atmosphere - hotter than the photosphere, a real puzzle disclosed briefly.

Press Core, Radiative, Convective, Photosphere, Corona, or All to highlight each layer. Click shells in the 3D view for the same facts panel.
