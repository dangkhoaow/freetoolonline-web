Chasmosaurus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

This page renders a Chasmosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the frill, a brow horn, or a shoulder to read a fact about that part.

Chasmosaurus was a Late Cretaceous ceratopsian from the Dinosaur Park Formation of Alberta, Canada. Lawrence Lambe named the genus in 1914. Published length estimates commonly fall around 4.3 to 5 m; mass estimates span about 1.5 to 2.5 tonnes - this viewer uses about 4.8 m and 2 t as mid-range panel values within those ranges. Its skull is famous for a large, fenestrated frill with openings, and brow horns that are typically smaller than those of Triceratops.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue; this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
