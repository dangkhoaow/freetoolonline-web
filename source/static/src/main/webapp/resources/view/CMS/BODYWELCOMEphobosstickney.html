Phobos Stickney Crater 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Phobos is the larger of Mars's two moons: an irregular body about 27 by 22 by 18 kilometers across that races around the planet three times each Martian day. Its most famous scar is Stickney crater - roughly 9 kilometers wide - named for Chloe Angeline Stickney Hall, mathematician and wife of Asaph Hall, who discovered both Martian moons in 1877. Stickney takes up a huge fraction of one face; Mars Global Surveyor later showed fine dust and landslide textures on its walls.

Across Phobos runs a network of grooves and crater chains, typically 100 to 200 meters wide and about 20 meters deep. Early ideas linked them to Stickney's impact stresses; later work also points to ejecta from Mars impacts or tidal stretching. This explorer does not pick a single origin story - it shows the crater bowl and a schematic groove network so you can see the scale relationship.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

This is an educational visualization - Phobos is smoothed into an ellipsoid, Stickney is a schematic bowl, and grooves are exaggerated lines rather than meter-accurate topography. Groove origin remains debated in the literature.
