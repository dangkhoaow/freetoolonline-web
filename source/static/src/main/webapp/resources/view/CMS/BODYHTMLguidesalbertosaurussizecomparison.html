How the Albertosaurus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Albertosaurus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 9 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Albertosaurus

What a person would see is a lightly built tyrannosaurid whose panel length is 9 m - shorter than a 1.8 m person end-to-end - with hip height about 3.4 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how a Late Cretaceous Alberta tyrannosaurid compares to you.


What the Albertosaurus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a blended composite: the panel headline figures (9 m / 3.4 m hip / 2.2 t) are never merged with the German juvenile range (about 0.7-0.9 m / 0.3-0.6 kg) or treated as if every specimen matched the French adult. Soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. For the control walkthrough see how to view Albertosaurus in 3D.
