When to Play Claudicus Quest

Claudicus Quest is a turn-based medieval militia RPG that runs in a browser tab with local save and no account. It fits a 20 - 45 minute session across Brannford, Thornwood, and Greymoor Crypt. Here is when it is the right pick.

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

When you want a compact RPG with saves

The whole bundle is ~132 KB (~105 KB game.js). Saves land in ftol:claudicusquest:save; Continue on the title screen resumes your run. Pick Claudicus Quest when you want questing and gear without a large download.

When turn-based menu combat fits your mood

Fights use numbered menus (Attack, Defend, Potion, Flee, special) instead of real-time aiming. Good when you want to read the combat log and plan the next turn on a desktop keyboard.

When you have a keyboard handy

WASD movement, number keys in combat, and I / Q / M shortcuts are keyboard-first. There are no on-screen touch buttons, so a laptop or desktop is the practical device.

When privacy and no CDN matter

Everything runs client-side after the one-time load: no sign-in, no multiplayer, zero server calls during play, and no runtime CDN. Sprites and audio are drawn and synthesized in Canvas 2D and Web Audio.

When it is not the right pick

Skip it if you need touch controls and a larger town sandbox (try Violence Town at ~1.1 MB with tap pathing). Skip it if you want fifteen maps and gear panels (see Pixel Realm RPG at ~3.8 MB).
