Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Qianzhousaurus called Pinocchio rex?

Its snout is unusually long and narrow for a tyrannosaurid, making up about 70 percent of the skull length, unlike the short, deep snout of Tyrannosaurus rex. Early press coverage of the 2014 description nicknamed it Pinocchio rex for that long nose.

How big was Qianzhousaurus?

The holotype individual is estimated at about 6.3 m long and 2 m tall at the hip, weighing 750-757 kg. Because the holotype may not have been fully grown, some researchers estimate larger individuals could reach roughly 9 m. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow a standard tyrannosaurid body plan - the source model does not sculpt the exaggerated long snout in full detail - but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction regardless. Fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. The length, weight, and age figures shown are the real published values.

When and where did Qianzhousaurus live?

In the Late Cretaceous, roughly 72 to 66 million years ago, in what is now southeastern China. Junchang Lu and colleagues named it in 2014 from a nearly complete skull and partial skeleton found at a construction site near Ganzhou, in the Nanxiong Formation.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
