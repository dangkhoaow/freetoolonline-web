When to use Type Ia Supernova Standard Candle 3D Explorer

Use this explorer when you want to show why a white dwarf explodes at nearly the same mass every time, and how astronomers turn that near-uniform brightness into a measured distance.

  • Teaching why the Chandrasekhar limit sets off the explosion
  • Explaining the distance-modulus formula with a live, adjustable example
  • Showing why Type Ia supernovae are called a "standard candle" in cosmology

This is an educational visualization of the accretion-then-explosion mechanism, not a hydrodynamic simulation.
