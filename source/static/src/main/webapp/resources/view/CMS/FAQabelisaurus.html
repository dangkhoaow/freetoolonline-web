Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the body reconstructed if only a skull is known?

Abelisaurus fossils include a partial skull, not a complete skeleton. Museums and researchers routinely restore missing parts from close relatives. This viewer follows that practice using abelisaurid anatomy from genera such as Carnotaurus and Majungasaurus, and the copy states that limitation plainly.

How big was Abelisaurus?

Published estimates vary because the skeleton is incomplete. Length is often cited around 7.2 to 11 m and mass around 1.4 to 3 t. The facts panel uses about 7.4 m and about 2 t as mid-range values within those ranges. Turn on the human figure to compare against a 1.8 m person.

When and where did it live?

In the Late Cretaceous, about 83 to 80 million years ago, in what is now Argentina (Anacleto Formation).

How is Abelisaurus related to Carnotaurus or Majungasaurus?

All three are abelisaurid theropods with deep skulls and very short forelimbs. Carnotaurus is from South America and has paired brow horns; Majungasaurus is from Madagascar with a single skull-roof horn. Abelisaurus is older and known mainly from skull material from Argentina.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The numbers follow published ranges, but the full-body shape and colors are artistic. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton or a verified color restoration.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
