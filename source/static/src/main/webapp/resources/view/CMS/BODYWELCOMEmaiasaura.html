Rotate, walk around, and scale this Maiasaura (Maiasaura peeblesorum), a 9-meter duck-billed hadrosaur (herbivore) that lived 75 million years ago in the Late Cretaceous. Maiasaura means "good mother" - fossils show they nested in herds and cared for their young.

Size: 9 - 10 meters long, 4 - 5 meters tall, 2,300 - 3,500 kg.
When: 75 million years ago (Late Cretaceous, Campanian).
Where: Montana, USA (Egg Mountain fossil site, first discovered 1979).

Special Feature Nesting behavior; herds with eggs and young

Nesting colony: The Egg Mountain dig (discovered by Jack Horner) revealed extensive fossil evidence of Maiasaura nesting sites - thousands of eggs arranged in herds, fossil embryos inside eggs, juvenile skeletons of various sizes, and adult bones surrounding the nests. This is strong evidence that Maiasaura parents fed and cared for their young before they left the nest, unusual behavior for reptiles but common in birds.

Skin and color are an artistic reconstruction - fossils do not preserve skin color or exact surface texture. The model shows a plausible countershading (darker above, lighter below, seen in modern reptiles and birds) and a skin tone consistent with theropod lineage, but the exact pigmentation is interpretive.
