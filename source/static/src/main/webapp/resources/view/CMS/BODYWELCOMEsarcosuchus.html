Sarcosuchus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

This page renders a Sarcosuchus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a person beside it for scale, and click the head, the body, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Sarcosuchus was a giant prehistoric crocodyliform, nicknamed "SuperCroc," that lived in the Early Cretaceous, roughly 112 to 97 million years ago, in what is now Niger and Brazil. It was not a dinosaur - it was a distant crocodile relative - but it shared rivers and floodplains with the dinosaurs of its time and likely ambushed them from the water. Its fossils were recovered between 1946 and 1959 by Albert-Felix de Lapparent; Paul Sereno described the more complete specimens in 1997 and 2000, popularizing the "SuperCroc" name.

Current published estimates put its length around 9.5 m and its weight around 3.45-4.3 t; an older estimate, based on the single largest known skull, ran as high as 11.65 m and about 8 t. This page uses the current, more conservative figures as its panel baseline and shows the wider range here because sources disagree.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue; this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. Sarcosuchus is not related to the similarly outsized Mosasaurus 3D Viewer - one was a river-dwelling crocodile relative, the other an ocean-going marine reptile - but both share this site's "not actually a dinosaur, and honestly labeled as such" approach.
