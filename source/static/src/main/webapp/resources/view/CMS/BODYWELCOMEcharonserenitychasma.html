Charon Serenity Chasma 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Charon is Pluto's largest moon - about 1,212 kilometers across. When New Horizons flew by in July 2015, it revealed a belt of pull-apart tectonic faults cutting the encounter hemisphere. One prominent segment, informally called Serenity Chasma, is more than 200 kilometers long, typically 40 to 50 kilometers wide, and about 5 to 7 kilometers deep. The wider chasm system runs at least about 1,800 kilometers and reaches depths near 7.5 kilometers in places - far longer and deeper than Earth's Grand Canyon.

NASA scientists interpret the fractures as evidence that Charon once had a subsurface water ocean that later froze and expanded, stretching and cracking the icy crust. That ocean story is a scientific hypothesis grounded in the fault morphology - this explorer shows the canyon geometry and an optional schematic interior hint, not a thermal-evolution model.
