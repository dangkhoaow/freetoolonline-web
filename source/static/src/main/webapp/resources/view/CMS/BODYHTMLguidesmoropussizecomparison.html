How the Moropus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Moropus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 3.3 m panel baseline (within the published about 3-3.5 m range). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Moropus

What a person would see is a clawed quadruped whose panel length is about 3.3 m - roughly 1.8 body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with a shoulder line about 2.4 m high for large M. elatus. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a forelimb to feel how the claws sit relative to the scale figure.


What the Moropus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a land-dinosaur estimate: the Moropus size comparison uses published bone-based estimates for a Miocene chalicothere mammal - not a dinosaur - and soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction.
