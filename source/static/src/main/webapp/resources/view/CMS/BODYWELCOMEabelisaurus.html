Abelisaurus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

This page renders Abelisaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the skull, a short forelimb, or a hind leg to read a fact about that part.

Abelisaurus is known from a partial skull from the Anacleto Formation of Argentina. Named by Bonaparte and Novas in 1985, it was the first abelisaurid to receive a formal name. Published length estimates span about 7.2 to 11 m and mass about 1.4 to 3 t; this viewer uses about 7.4 m and about 2 t as mid-range values within those ranges. The body you see is inferred from close abelisaurid relatives - not from a complete Abelisaurus skeleton.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue; this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
