When the Deinonychus browser viewer fits

Pick the Deinonychus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a person-sized sickle-clawed predator, Size vs human at the honest 3.4 m length ratio, and click facts on head, body, neck, tail, leg, or arm - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures and the Jurassic Park size mythbust.


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Deinonychus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Deinonychus comparison is not

This Deinonychus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise weight beyond the cited 60-100 kg range, it does not claim Deinonychus is Velociraptor by name, it does not claim pack-hunting is confirmed, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
