Why Use a PDF Page Trimmer?

Pulling a page range out of a PDF is handy whenever you only need part of a longer document. Common cases:

  • Send part of a contract: share just the signature pages instead of the full agreement.
  • Pull key pages from a report: extract the summary or a single appendix for review or quoting.
  • Save a chapter: keep one section of an e-book or research paper as its own file.
  • Trim for delivery: dropping pages outside the range usually leaves a smaller file to attach or upload.

FAQ: PDF Page Trimming and Splitting

How do I extract more than one range at once?

Enter the ranges comma-separated, for example 1-3,7,10-12. That produces three output files: pages 1-3, page 7, and pages 10-12. A single range like 3-7 returns one file.

Does it keep bookmarks inside the range?

Generally yes - bookmarks that point to a page within the range are kept. Bookmarks that point outside the range are dropped, since the page they link to is no longer in the new file.

Can I split a password-protected PDF?

Only if you have the password. Unlock the file first with Remove PDF Password, then run it through this tool.

Does extracting a range change the quality of the kept pages?

No. The pages inside the range are carried over as they are, with their original resolution and text intact. Only the pages outside the range are left out.
