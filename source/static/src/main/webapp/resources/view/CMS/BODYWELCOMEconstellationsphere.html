Constellation Sphere 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Constellation Sphere 3D Explorer shows a teaching celestial sphere with embedded bright-star RA hours, Dec degrees, and V magnitudes, classic asterism sticks (Orion, Ursa Major, Crux, Summer Triangle), and the IAU count of 88 official constellations.

Drag to orbit, scroll or pinch to zoom, tap Orion / Ursa Major / Crux / Summer Triangle to highlight sticks, toggle the equatorial teaching grid, and use Mag limit to show brighter-only samples. Click a star for its catalog-style figure - Sirius reads about -1.46 V in the panel.

The facts panel keeps the honesty line: asterism sticks and a bright-star sample, not full IAU boundary polygons or an all-sky survey download.

  • Celestial sphere shell with equatorial teaching grid toggle
  • IAU constellation count: 88
  • Embedded bright-star RA hours, Dec degrees, V mag
  • Orion, Ursa Major, Crux, and Summer Triangle asterism focus buttons
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Teachers use Constellation Sphere 3D Explorer to show how RA/Dec place stars on a sphere, students connect stick figures to named bright stars, and curious readers separate asterisms from the official 88 IAU regions.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

Constellation Sphere 3D Explorer is not a full IAU boundary polygon atlas, not an all-sky survey texture or photometric catalog dump, and not a planetarium with live time or telescope control. For the Sun path and 13 ecliptic constellations, open Ecliptic Zodiac 3D Explorer; for apparent versus absolute brightness, open Stellar Magnitude 3D Explorer.
