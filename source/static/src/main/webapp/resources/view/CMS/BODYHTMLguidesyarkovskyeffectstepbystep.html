How to Explore the Yarkovsky Effect 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Yarkovsky Effect 3D Explorer, pick a rotation sense with the selector, and scrub or play the elapsed-years slider while a spinning, irregular asteroid's thermal-lag hot spot pushes its orbit ring in or out.

  1. Open /space-3d/yarkovsky-effect.html and wait for the asteroid, the Sun marker, and the orbit ring to render.
  2. Use the Rotation selector to pick Retrograde (Bennu, measured) or Prograde (teaching mirror).
  3. Drag the Elapsed years slider (0-100) or click "Play / Pause Years" to watch the orbit ring shrink (retrograde) or grow (prograde).
  4. Watch the recoil-thrust arrow at the hot spot - red when the push shrinks the orbit, green when it grows the orbit.
  5. Read the facts panel for the current rotation's signed rate, the cumulative-drift readout, and Bennu's real measured figures.
  6. Switch rotation sense and compare the two orbit-ring animations side by side.
