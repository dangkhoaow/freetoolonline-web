Pluto-Charon Double Planet 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

This browser explorer shows Pluto and Charon as a near double-planet pair: both worlds orbit a shared barycenter that sits outside Pluto, and both stay tidally locked so the same faces keep looking at each other.

NASA New Horizons and JPL figures put Pluto's mean radius at 1188.3 km and Charon's at 606 km. Masses are about 1.303e22 kg and 1.586e21 kg - a mass ratio near 8.2:1. Center-to-center separation is about 19,591 km. The barycenter lies about 2,110 km from Pluto's center, which exceeds Pluto's 1188.3 km radius, so the barycenter is outside the primary. The synchronous orbital period is 6.387 days.

Orbital-mechanics teachers use the outside-Pluto barycenter to show why this pair is the classic near double-planet case. Readers who already know Earth-Moon geometry use this page for the mutual lock and exterior barycenter that the Earth-Moon explorer does not teach.
