This page renders a Mapusaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, an arm, the tail, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Mapusaurus roseae lived in the Late Cretaceous, roughly 97 to 93.5 million years ago (the Cenomanian to Turonian), in the Huincul Formation of Neuquen Province, Argentina. The Argentine-Canadian Dinosaur Project excavated a bonebed of seven to nine individuals at Canadon del Gato, south of Plaza Huincul, between 1997 and 2001. Rodolfo Coria and Philip J. Currie formally described and named the species in 2006 from this material, designating an isolated nasal bone (specimen MCF-PVPH-108.1) as the holotype, with twelve additional partial skeletons as paratypes.

Published length estimates for Mapusaurus range from about 10.2 m, based on the largest complete femur described in the original paper, to about 12.6 m in later studies; weight estimates run from about 6 to 8 tonnes. Because several individuals in the bonebed were found together, some researchers have suggested Mapusaurus may have hunted in groups, similar to its close relative Giganotosaurus - though a bonebed alone does not prove coordinated pack behavior, and the idea remains debated among paleontologists.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue, and this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton; the numbers above are real published values, with ranges shown because sources vary. Mapusaurus belongs to the same carcharodontosaurid family as the already-shipped Giganotosaurus 3D Viewer, but its bonebed is the strongest fossil evidence yet found for group behavior in a giant predatory dinosaur.
