Frequently Asked Questions

How big is Herschel compared with Mimas?

Herschel is about 130-140 km across on a moon about 396 km across - roughly one third of one Mimas face. That relative size is why the impact is often described as nearly moon-shattering.

Why does it look like the Death Star?

The large circular basin with a bright central peak resembles the fictional battle station. NASA and others note the likeness, but Voyager imaged Herschel in 1980 - after the 1977 film - so the match is coincidence, not design.

What do Play, Peak, and Speed do?

Play spins the moon. Peak shows or hides the schematic central mountain. Speed changes how fast the spin runs. Drag to orbit and scroll or pinch to zoom.

Is this a real Cassini terrain model?

No. It is a procedural schematic grounded in published sizes. Depths and peak height are exaggerated for visibility. It does not solve impact physics or map every groove.

Does Mimas have an ocean?

Some Cassini libration and later studies discuss a possible young ocean or elongated core. The evidence is debated. This explorer does not choose a side or draw an interior ocean layer.
