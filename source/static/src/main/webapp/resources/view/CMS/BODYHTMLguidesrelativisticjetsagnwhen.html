When to Use the Relativistic Jets & Doppler Beaming 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

Open this explorer when you want to see why a black hole's jet looks one-sided from Earth, or to teach Doppler beaming and apparent superluminal motion with the real M87 numbers - not to run a magnetohydrodynamic jet-launching simulation.


A beaming lesson

A lesson starts at the reset baseline (no beaming) to see two equally bright jets, then loads the "M87" preset to watch one jet brighten and the other all but disappear, reading the computed Doppler factor and apparent speed off the facts panel at each step.


Sessions this explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are exploring the exact Kerr photon-orbit and ISCO geometry - use the Photon Sphere & ISCO 3D Explorer for that - or viewing an artistic accretion-disk glow with no beaming physics - use the Black Hole 3D Explorer. Walk the controls in the step-by-step guide.
