How the Titanoboa size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Titanoboa model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 12.8 m mean (within the published range up to about 14.3 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Titanoboa

What a person would see is a scale gap of roughly seven body lengths: from snout to tip a mean Titanoboa spans about 12.8 m, about 7.1 body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the mid-body to feel how far the coils extend past the scale figure while the animal sits on the water plane.


What the Titanoboa size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a land-dinosaur estimate: the Titanoboa size comparison uses published bone-based estimates for a giant snake - not a dinosaur - and soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction.
