How to Explore the Proxima Centauri System 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Proxima Centauri System 3D Explorer, watch the Sun's nearest star and its 3 known/candidate planets orbit at their real relative speeds, and use the habitable-zone toggle and flare button to see why this system's habitability story is more complicated than "closest star, has a planet".

  1. Open /space-3d/proxima-centauri-system.html and wait for the red-orange star, its 3 orbiting planets, and the green habitable-zone ring to render.
  2. Press "Pause" to stop the orbital motion, or press it again to resume.
  3. Drag the speed slider (0.2x to 3x) to slow down or speed up how fast the planets orbit.
  4. Click "Habitable zone: on" to toggle the translucent green band marking the conservative habitable-zone estimate (0.042-0.082 AU).
  5. Click "Trigger a flare" to play a roughly 1.6-second stellar-flare pulse that brightens the star and sends an expanding ring out across the whole system.
  6. Read the facts panel table for each planet's real distance, orbital period, and mass.
