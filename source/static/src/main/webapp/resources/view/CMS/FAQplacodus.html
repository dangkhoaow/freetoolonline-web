Frequently Asked Questions

Was Placodus a dinosaur?

No. Placodus was a marine reptile - a placodont - that lived alongside the earliest dinosaurs but belongs to a separate group. This viewer includes it because placodonts are a distinctive, frequently searched part of Triassic marine life.

How big was Placodus?

Published length estimates run about 2 to 3 m, with most sources citing 2.5-3 m. No rigorous scientific mass estimate exists; the roughly 227 kg (500 lb) figure sometimes quoted is a popular estimate, not a peer-reviewed calculation.

Why did Placodus have such unusual teeth?

Its name means "plate tooth". Broad, flat teeth lined the roof of its mouth and lower jaw, forming crushing plates for shellfish, while a few forward-pointing chisel-like front teeth likely pried mollusks loose from rocks.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body shape follows fossil reconstructions - a stocky trunk on four legs, with a long tail - but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction. Fossils preserve bone, not color. The figures shown are real published values.
