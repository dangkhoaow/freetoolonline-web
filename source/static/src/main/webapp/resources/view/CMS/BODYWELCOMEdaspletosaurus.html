This page renders a Daspletosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, a tiny arm, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Daspletosaurus lived in the Late Cretaceous, about 76 to 74 million years ago, in Alberta, Canada. The Natural History Museum lists length about 9.0 m; adult mass is commonly cited about 2-3 tonnes (viewer uses 2500 kg). It was a carnivorous tyrannosaurine relative of Tyrannosaurus rex. Named by Dale Russell in 1970 (type D. torosus).
