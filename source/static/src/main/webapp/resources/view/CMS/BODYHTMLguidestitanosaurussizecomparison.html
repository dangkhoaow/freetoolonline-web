How the Titanosaurus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Titanosaurus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 10.5 m panel baseline (within the published about 9-12 m range). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Titanosaurus

What a person would see is a sauropod whose panel length is about 10.5 m - roughly 5.8 body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 3.2 m high in the panel. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind limb to feel how far the neck and tail extend past the scale figure.


What the Titanosaurus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single settled mass: the Titanosaurus size comparison keeps the published 4-13 t range (panel 5.5 t) and notes that the genus is often treated as a nomen dubium.
