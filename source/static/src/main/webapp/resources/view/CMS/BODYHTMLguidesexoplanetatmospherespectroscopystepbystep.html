How to Explore Exoplanet Atmosphere Spectroscopy 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Exoplanet Atmosphere Spectroscopy 3D Explorer, toggle a molecule button, and scrub the transit-progress slider to watch the wavelength-by-wavelength absorption signal from WASP-39b appear only while the planet crosses its star.

  1. Open /space-3d/exoplanet-atmosphere-spectroscopy.html and wait for the star and planet to render.
  2. Press "Water (H2O)", "Carbon dioxide (CO2)" or "Sulfur dioxide (SO2)" to turn on that molecule's real absorption bands on the spectrum panel.
  3. Drag the transit-progress slider from 0 toward mid-transit and watch the absorption bumps grow, then fade back outside the transit window.
  4. Press "Jump to mid-transit" to snap straight to the point of maximum atmospheric signal.
  5. Press "Play orbit" to run the transit automatically.
  6. Read the facts panel for WASP-39b's published figures and each molecule's real detection status.
