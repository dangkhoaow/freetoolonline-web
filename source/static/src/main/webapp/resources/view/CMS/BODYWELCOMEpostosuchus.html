Postosuchus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

This page renders a Postosuchus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a person beside it for scale, and click the head, the body, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Postosuchus was a rauisuchian pseudosuchian, a crocodile-line archosaur that lived in the Late Triassic, roughly 227 to 208 million years ago, in what is now Texas and North Carolina. It was not a dinosaur - it belongs to a separate branch of archosaurs - but it was among the largest predators of its time, tens of millions of years before the first tyrannosaurs. Sankar Chatterjee described it in 1985 from fossils found at Post Quarry, Texas.

Published length estimates span about 4 to 7 m and mass estimates span about 250 to 1000 kg because complete skeletons are rare and reconstructions differ. This page uses about 5.5 m and about 500 kg as its panel baseline and shows the wider ranges here because sources disagree. Whether Postosuchus walked mainly on four legs or two remains an open research question; the procedural model shows a cautious quadrupedal stance and the page says so plainly.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue; this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. Postosuchus is not related to the similarly honest Dimetrodon 3D Viewer - one is a Triassic archosaur, the other an earlier sail-backed synapsid - but both share this site's approach of labeling non-dinosaurs clearly.
