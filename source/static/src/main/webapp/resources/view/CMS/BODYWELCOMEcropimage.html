Looking for a quick and effective way to edit your images? Our online tool offers in-browser cropping and rotation that preserves the source format, ideal for adjusting photos in seconds, without needing any software downloads.

Image cropper and rotator trims an image to a specific aspect ratio and rotates it in 45-degree steps or at a free angle.

  1. Upload a JPG, PNG, or WebP image.
  2. Drag the crop box to your target frame, or pick an aspect-ratio preset; rotate with the controls if needed.
  3. Download the cropped image.

Tip: Aspect-ratio presets cover the common social sizes (1:1, 4:5, 16:9) and avoid manual pixel math.

Picking JPG or PNG after the crop? Read JPG vs PNG for the web for the byte-count comparison that usually picks the winner: photographic crops save more bytes as JPG, while crops with flat colour, sharp edges, or transparency keep their fidelity as PNG. The guide also covers when WebP outperforms both at the same visual quality.

Five aspect-ratio presets (16:9, 4:3, 1:1, 2:3, Free) cover the common social and print sizes. The original image stays in the workspace, so a second crop at a different ratio takes one click instead of a re-upload. Every crop runs inside the browser via HTML canvas - the picture is never uploaded to a server, never persists past the page reload, and closing the tab drops the working copy. Useful when the source contains a face, a personal document, or other sensitive content. No account to create, no watermark, and no daily cap.

The zoom controls magnify the working canvas so the crop frame can land on a single eye, a label, or a corner. Switch the drag-mode toggle from Crop to Move to pan the magnified region, then flip back to Crop to draw the frame. A live preview pane shows the result at the natural output size while the frame is being dragged - no need to commit and re-crop. Cropping is one kind of resize: the Free preset and pixel side inputs let you land an exact pixel footprint for landing-page or marketplace listings that demand a specific frame. For the opposite case - keeping the full frame and scaling the entire image proportionally - the dedicated tool is resize image online free; this page handles the framing-and-cut decision only.

The crop output preserves the source format: JPG comes out as JPG, PNG as PNG (transparency intact), WebP as WebP - no re-encoding, no quality re-compression. If the next step is a format switch, that conversion is a separate trip to image converter tools.

Need to straighten a sideways shot first? The Rotate Left and Rotate Right buttons turn the image in 45-degree steps; drag the rotation handle for a free-angle correction on a tilted horizon. The crop frame stays anchored to the rotated content so the final cut tracks the new orientation.
