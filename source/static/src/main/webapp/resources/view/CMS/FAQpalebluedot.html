Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Pale Blue Dot?

A photograph of Earth taken by Voyager 1 on 14 February 1990, from about 40.5 AU away, part of a 60-frame "Family Portrait" of the Solar System.

How small does Earth look in the photo?

Less than a single pixel - about 0.12 of a pixel, caught inside a scattered ray of reflected sunlight.

Whose idea was the photo?

Voyager imaging scientist Carl Sagan championed the idea; JPL scientists Candy Hansen and Carolyn Porco designed the exact command sequence.

What happened right after the photo was taken?

Just 34 minutes later, Voyager 1 powered off its cameras permanently, to save power for its ongoing journey out of the Solar System.

What is the famous quote about the photo?

Carl Sagan, in his 1994 book named after the image: "Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us."

How far away was Voyager 1 when it took the photo?

About 40.5 astronomical units - roughly 3.7 billion miles, or 6 billion kilometers.

Is this scene to scale?

No. The Sun, Earth, and Voyager model are enlarged for visibility, and the recreated photo layout is illustrative, not a pixel-accurate reproduction of the real photograph.
