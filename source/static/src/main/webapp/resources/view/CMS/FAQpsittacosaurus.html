Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Psittacosaurus?

Published estimates vary by species and age, but adults are often cited around 1.9 to 2.5 m long and about 9 to 20 kg. The facts panel uses about 2 m and about 20 kg as mid-range values. Turn on the human figure to compare against a 1.8 m person.

Did it have a frill or horns like Triceratops?

No. Psittacosaurus is an early ceratopsian with a parrot-like beak but without the large frill and horns seen in later relatives such as Triceratops.

When and where did it live?

In the Early Cretaceous, about 126 to 101 million years ago, in what is now Asia (Mongolia, China, and related formations).

Did Psittacosaurus have feathers or quills?

Some fossils preserve bristle-like integument, especially on the tail. That evidence supports some kind of filamentous covering in life, but color and exact arrangement are not known. This viewer uses a simplified scaly reconstruction and does not draw individual quills.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The numbers follow published ranges, but the body proportions, colors, and surface detail are artistic. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton or a verified color restoration.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
