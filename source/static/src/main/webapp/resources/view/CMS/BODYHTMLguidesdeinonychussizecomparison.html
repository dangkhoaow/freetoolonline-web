Deinonychus Size Comparison

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Deinonychus 3D Viewer pairs published Early Cretaceous dromaeosaurid figures with a 1.8 m person on screen - length about 3.3-3.4 m (Size vs human uses 3.4 m), hip height roughly 0.87 m, and weight about 60-100 kg - so the scale gap stays readable at a glance.

How the Deinonychus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Deinonychus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio used by the viewer (3.4 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker. Published length still spans about 3.3-3.4 m, so the toggle picks one clear scale inside that disclosed range.


What a person would see beside a Deinonychus

What a person would see is a bipedal, sickle-clawed predator roughly person-sized end-to-end - turn on the person in the viewer and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how the body sits relative to the scale figure. Skin color is an artistic reconstruction; feathering is inferred from related dromaeosaurids and left unrendered; pack behavior from Tenontosaurus evidence is only suggested, not confirmed.


What the Deinonychus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a claim of one precise weight: the Deinonychus size comparison discloses the about 3.3-3.4 m, 0.87 m hip, and 60-100 kg ranges and uses 3.4 m only for the Size vs human scale; it does not invent a settled pack-hunting claim, does not claim Deinonychus is Velociraptor by name, no free-licensed Deinonychus glTF is re-hosted yet so the viewer stays procedural, and this is not a win/lose game.
