Como ver Lambeosaurus em 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Revisado pela equipe editorial FreetoolOnline

Lambeosaurus 3D Viewer runs a Late Cretaceous hollow-crested hadrosaur in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the head, hatchet-shaped crest, tail, or a leg for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Lambeosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Lambeosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. Orbit with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen keeps the controls visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean glTF may swap in from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural silhouette.


Use the four Lambeosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Lambeosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (9.5 m)

Read the Lambeosaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Lambeosaurus facts panel lists length about 9.5 m (panel mid-range; published range about 9-15 m), height about 4 m, weight about 5 t (published range about 4-6 t), era 76-75 Mya Late Cretaceous Campanian, herbivore diet, named by William Parks in 1923 honoring Lawrence Lambe from Dinosaur Park Formation Alberta Canada. The hollow hatchet or axe-shaped bony crest is a third crest shape on this site beside Parasaurolophus tube crest and Saurolophus spike crest. Wide length range reflects taxonomic revision across historically separate named species now treated as growth stages or synonyms. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. Procedural first paint uses a simplified cone crest; the real hatchet shape appears after an optional CC0/Public Domain glTF (Model Magic via Printables) swaps in. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Lambeosaurus how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not claim a single settled length outside the published 9-15 m range, it does not claim the procedural cone crest is anatomically exact, and it does not invent game score or win states.
