Kentrosaurus 3D Viewer

This page renders a Kentrosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a person beside it for scale, and click the head, a plate, a tail spike, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Kentrosaurus lived in the Late Jurassic, about 152 to 151 million years ago, in what is now Tanzania. Thousands of its bones were excavated between 1909 and 1912 by German expeditions at the Tendaguru fossil beds; most of the surviving material, roughly 350 specimens, is held at the Museum fur Naturkunde in Berlin (many original specimens were destroyed in the Second World War). The genus was named by Edwin Hennig in 1915; the name means "spiky lizard."

Kentrosaurus was smaller than its better-known relative Stegosaurus but more heavily armed for its size: plates run along the neck and front half of the back, then give way to at least seven pairs of long, paired spikes over the hips and tail. Published length estimates for typical adults run about 4 to 4.5 m, with the largest individual specimens reported up to about 5.5 m; mass estimates span a wide range, roughly 700 to 1,600 kg, reflecting how differently researchers have modeled its build.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone, plate color, and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue, and this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton; the numbers above are real published values, and ranges are shown because sources vary.
