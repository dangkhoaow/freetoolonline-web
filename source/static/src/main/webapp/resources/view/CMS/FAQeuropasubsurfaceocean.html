Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Europa Subsurface Ocean 3D Explorer show?

It peels back Jupiter's moon Europa layer by layer - ice shell, ocean, rocky mantle, and metallic core - so you can see what published planetary science places underneath the ice.

How thick is Europa's ice shell and ocean?

NASA JPL estimates the ice shell at about 15-25 km thick over a liquid-water ocean about 60-150 km deep, believed to hold roughly twice the water of all of Earth's oceans combined.

Are the layer thicknesses shown to scale?

No. On-screen layer thicknesses are exaggerated so the ice shell and ocean stay visible - at true scale they are under 2 percent of Europa's radius. The facts panel carries the real published ranges.

Is this a physical simulation of Europa's interior?

No. It is an educational visualization tuned to teach the layer order - it does not compute a geophysical model of density, pressure, or heat flow.

What are the Clipper flyby tracks?

Six illustrative ground-track arcs on the ice shell, toggled on with the Clipper flyby tracks checkbox. They are schematic, not NASA's real mission-planned flyby paths.

How do I cut through to the core?

Slide Peel shell toward 100 percent, or press the Ice shell, Ocean, Mantle, and Core buttons to jump straight to that layer's cutaway.
