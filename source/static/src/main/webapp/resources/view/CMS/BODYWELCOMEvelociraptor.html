This page renders a Velociraptor as a 3D model you can spin: drag to rotate, pinch to zoom, add a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body part for a fact.

Velociraptor mongoliensis lived in the Late Cretaceous, roughly 75-71 million years ago, in Mongolia's Gobi Desert - about 2 m long, 0.5 m tall, 15-20 kg: closer to a turkey than the human-sized movie predator. A curved claw over 6.5 cm long tipped its second toe. The first fossil was found in 1923; the genus was named in 1924. In 2007, researchers found quill knobs (feather-anchor bumps) on a forearm bone - real evidence this dinosaur carried feathers the model does not render.
