How the Brontotherium size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Brontotherium model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 4.6 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Brontotherium

What a person would see is a bulky horned brontothere mammal whose panel length is about 4.6 m - a little more than two and a half body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with shoulders about 2.5 m high. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the blunt Y-shaped nasal horns to feel the mammal-line scale, not a dinosaur outline.


What the Brontotherium size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays the disclosed about 2-5 tonnes range with panel baseline 3.5 tonnes.
