Frequently Asked Questions

What is Googol Stopping Game?

A free browser decision puzzle: fifty numbered tiles hide huge numbers. You reveal tiles one by one and must decide when to stop, hoping your last pick is the biggest.

How do I play?

Press Play, then click or tap a numbered tile to reveal its value. Your last revealed value is your choice. Press I'm done when you think you have the biggest. Press Computer to watch an automated 1/e sample strategy.

What does Computer do?

It resets the board, samples about n/e tiles to learn a threshold, then keeps picking until it sees a larger value - a classic optimal-stopping heuristic.

Is progress saved?

No. This build is session-only with no localStorage saves.

How big is the download?

About ~8 KB of HTML, JavaScript, CSS, and a small favicon after you press Play.

Does it work on a phone?

Yes. Tiles and buttons are click/tap targets, so phones and desktops both work.

Is this open source?

Yes. Adapted from googol by Victor Ribeiro under the MIT license. This site build strips the service worker and install prompt, adds noindex, and ships LICENSE plus CREDITS next to the engine.
