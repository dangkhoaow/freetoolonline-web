Triton Cantaloupe Terrain 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Triton is the largest moon of Neptune - about 2,700 kilometers across in NASA summaries, with Voyager 2 refining the diameter near 2,706 kilometers. It is the only large Solar System moon on a retrograde orbit. Voyager 2 flew past on 25 August 1989 and returned the first close views of cantaloupe terrain.

That landscape fills much of Triton's western hemisphere: closely spaced dimples (cavi) about 30 to 40 kilometers across, with crosscutting ridges. Leading ideas include diapirism or stresses from global cooling. This explorer shows a schematic dimple grid without picking one origin story, and it does not model nitrogen geysers.
