Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Suchomimus?

Published estimates put it at about 9.5 to 11 m long and roughly 2.5 to 5.2 tonnes. The holotype may represent a subadult, so some studies argue for an adult size as large as 11 to 12 m. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

What was the low ridge on its back for?

A row of short neural spines forms a low ridge along the back - shorter than the tall sail seen on the related Spinosaurus. Researchers are not certain of its function; possibilities include display or temperature regulation, similar to the ideas proposed for taller-sailed relatives.

How is Suchomimus different from Baryonyx and Spinosaurus?

All three are spinosaurids with long, croc-like, fish-eating snouts, but they differ. Suchomimus carries a low ridge of short spines, while Spinosaurus carries a tall sail of much taller spines. Suchomimus is also larger than Baryonyx (roughly 9.5-11 m against about 7.5 m). Each comes from a different place and time: Suchomimus from the Elrhaz Formation in Niger (121-112 million years ago), Baryonyx from the Wealden Formation in England, and Spinosaurus from the Bahariya Formation in Egypt.

What did Suchomimus eat?

Its long, low, crocodile-like skull and numerous small, conical, unserrated teeth are suited to gripping slippery prey rather than slicing meat, pointing to a mostly piscivorous (fish-eating) diet - the same feeding strategy inferred for its close relatives Baryonyx and Spinosaurus.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, skull length, and age figures shown are real published values.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
