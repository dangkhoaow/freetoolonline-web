Explore the most volcanically active body in our Solar System - Jupiter's moon Io - with real coordinates and eruption data from NASA's Juno spacecraft. Click the red hotspots on Io's surface to view each volcano's temperature, eruption height, and the NASA missions that confirmed their locations.

  • No account needed: 100% browser-based, runs fully offline after first load

Why Io Has So Many Volcanoes

Io orbits so close to Jupiter (420,000 km) that the planet's gravitational pull creates intense tidal heating. This internal friction powers more than 500 active volcanoes - more than all of Earth's volcanoes combined. The explorer shows the most significant ones confirmed by NASA telescopes and spacecraft.

How the 3D Explorer Works

The explorer runs 100% in your browser using WebGL and the open-source three.js library. Io is rendered as a procedurally-generated sphere with real sulfur-yellow surface colors. Each volcano is marked with a red hotspot positioned at its real IAU-registered coordinates. When you click a hotspot, the actual NASA-published temperature and height data appears. Drag to rotate Io, scroll to zoom - no downloads, no server uploads, no plugins needed.

Privacy & Performance

This explorer is entirely client-side - no data is sent to any server. Your browser downloads the 3D scene once (about 250 KB) and caches it for offline use. Works on desktop, tablet, and mobile. Average first paint: under 2 seconds on standard broadband.
