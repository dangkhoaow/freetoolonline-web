Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Daspletosaurus?

The Natural History Museum lists length about 9.0 m. Adult mass is commonly cited about 2-3 tonnes; this viewer uses 2500 kg. Standing height is about 2.8 m at the hips/shoulders for scale consistency. Turn on the human figure to judge the scale beside a 1.8 m person.

How is Daspletosaurus different from Tyrannosaurus rex?

Daspletosaurus was a tyrannosaurine relative of T. rex from Alberta, Canada, living about 76-74 Mya - earlier and typically smaller than the later, bulkier T. rex. Both were large-headed carnivores with tiny arms.

Where did Daspletosaurus live?

Fossils come from Alberta, Canada, in the Late Cretaceous about 76-74 million years ago.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body proportions follow published reconstructions, but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction. Fossils preserve bone, not color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The figures shown are real published values with ranges where sources disagree.

When was Daspletosaurus named?

Dale Russell named Daspletosaurus in 1970; the type species is D. torosus.
