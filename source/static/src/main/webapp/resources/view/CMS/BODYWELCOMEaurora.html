Aurora 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Aurora 3D Explorer shows the auroral oval expand as teaching Kp rises from 0 to 9, and matches curtain colours to emission altitudes - green oxygen 557.7 nm (about 90-150 km), red oxygen 630.0 nm (about 200-300 km), and blue/purple N2+ 427.8 nm at the lower fringe.

Drag to orbit the night-side Earth, step the Kp - / Kp + slider, and pause spin when you want a still curtain against the facts panel. Reset returns the demo to teaching Kp 3 so a quiet oval sits near 65-70 deg magnetic latitude before you storm-expand it.

The facts panel cites the same altitude bands and quiet oval latitudes used in NOAA SWPC OVATION literacy so the colour-vs-height takeaway stays reliable - geometry is compressed for readability.

  • Altitude envelope about 80-300 km
  • Green O 557.7 nm peak about 90-150 km
  • Red O 630.0 nm peak about 200-300 km
  • Quiet oval about 65-70 deg magnetic latitude; expands equatorward as Kp rises
  • Kp teaching slider 0-9 with reset to Kp 3
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Teachers use Aurora 3D Explorer for storm-expansion and emission-line demos, students connect Kp to oval width, and curious readers separate green/red/purple bands without a planetarium install.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

Aurora 3D Explorer is an educational oval and emission-altitude visualization - it is not a live NOAA SWPC forecast or real-time magnetometer feed, not a particle-precipitation solver, and not an observing-site trip planner. For geography and day/night lighting without curtains, use Earth 3D Globe.
