Frequently Asked Questions

How big was a real Woolly Mammoth?

Adults are commonly cited at up to about 3.4 m at the shoulder and roughly 6,000 kg, comparable to a modern African elephant. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a person.

Was the Woolly Mammoth a dinosaur?

No. The Woolly Mammoth was a mammal, related to modern elephants, that lived during the Pleistocene and early Holocene Ice Age - tens of millions of years after the dinosaurs went extinct.

How long were its tusks?

Curved tusks commonly reached past 3 m long, with the largest recorded specimens measuring around 4 m along the outer curve.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body proportions follow the fossil skeleton and frozen mummies found in permafrost, which preserve real mammoth hair and skin - unlike almost any dinosaur. Even so, this specific 3D model is an artistic sculpt, not a scan of an actual mummy. The height, weight, and age figures shown are real published values.

When and where did it live, and when did it go extinct?

Across the mammoth steppe of Eurasia and North America, roughly 400,000 to 4,000 years ago. Most died out around 10,000 years ago, but a population survived on Wrangel Island in the Arctic Ocean until about 4,000 years ago - contemporaries of the ancient Egyptian pyramids.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
