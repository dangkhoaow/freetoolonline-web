Amargasaurus 3D Viewer

This page renders an Amargasaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, neck, body, a leg, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Amargasaurus was a dicraeosaurid sauropod from the Early Cretaceous of what is now Neuquen, Argentina, in the La Amarga Formation. It was smaller than classic whip-tailed giants like Diplodocus or Barosaurus, with a shorter neck typical of dicraeosaurids. Its most striking feature is two parallel rows of tall neural spines running along the neck - researchers still debate whether those spines supported a sail-like membrane of skin or stood as bony cores for keratin sheaths; this page treats that question as open rather than settled.

Leonardo Salgado and Jose Bonaparte named the genus in 1991 from holotype MACN-N 15. Published length estimates span about 9 to 13 m and mass estimates about 2.5 to 4 t; this viewer uses about 10 m and about 3.2 t as mid-range panel values within those ranges.
