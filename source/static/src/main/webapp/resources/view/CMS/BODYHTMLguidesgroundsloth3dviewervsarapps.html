When the Ground Sloth browser viewer fits

Pick the Ground Sloth browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a Pleistocene mammal - not a dinosaur - with Size vs human at the honest 6 m length ratio, and click facts on head, body, neck, tail, or leg - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures and the optional CC-BY 3.0 model credit.


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside giant ground-sloth mounts and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Ground Sloth comparison is not

This Ground Sloth comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length or weight beyond about 6 m / 3,700-4,000 kg, it does not claim Megatherium was a dinosaur, it does not revive the inaccurate long-tongue depiction, it does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction, and it does not invent game score or win states.
