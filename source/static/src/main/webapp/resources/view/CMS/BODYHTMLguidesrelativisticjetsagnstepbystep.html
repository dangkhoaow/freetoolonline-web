How to Use the Relativistic Jets & Doppler Beaming 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Relativistic Jets & Doppler Beaming 3D Explorer, drag the two sliders, and watch the approaching jet brighten while the receding jet fades - computed live from the exact Doppler-beaming formulas.

  1. Open /space-3d/relativistic-jets-agn.html and wait for the black hole, the accretion disk, and the two bipolar jets to render.
  2. Drag the Lorentz factor slider (Γ = 1.01 to 15) to set how fast the jet plasma moves.
  3. Drag the viewing angle slider (θ = 1 to 90 degrees) to set the angle between the jet axis and your line of sight.
  4. Click "M87" to load the real VLBA-estimated values (Γ=10.9, θ=17 degrees) for the M87 jet.
  5. Click "Blazar-like" to see the extreme one-sided beaming a small viewing angle produces.
  6. Read the facts panel for the computed Doppler factors, the flux ratio between the two jets, and the apparent transverse speed.
