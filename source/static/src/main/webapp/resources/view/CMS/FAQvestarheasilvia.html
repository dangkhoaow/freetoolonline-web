Frequently Asked Questions

How big is Rheasilvia compared with Vesta?

About 500-505 kilometers across on a body with mean diameter near 529 kilometers - it covers most of the southern hemisphere.

How tall is the central peak?

Roughly 19 to 22 kilometers above the basin floor, with a complex about 200 kilometers wide - among the tallest mountains known in the Solar System.

What is Veneneia?

An older ~400 kilometer south-polar basin that Rheasilvia partly obliterated. This page shows only a faint rim hint.

When was Rheasilvia mapped?

Hubble saw the depression in 1997; Dawn mapped and named it in 2011.

Is the shape exact?

No. Dawn topography is far more irregular; the scene uses a smooth ellipsoid and schematic basin for readability.

Does this model Vestoid ejecta?

No. The copy notes the link to Vestoids and HED meteorites, but the scene does not animate ejecta streams.

Is this a physics simulation?

No. It is an educational visualization with compressed relief, not an impact hydrocode.
