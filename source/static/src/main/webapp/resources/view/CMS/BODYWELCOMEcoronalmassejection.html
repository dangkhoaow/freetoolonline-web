Coronal Mass Ejection 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

This browser explorer shows a coronal mass ejection as an expanding plasma cloud leaving the Sun's corona and crossing the compressed Sun-Earth gap, with travel time computed from the real 1 AU distance and your scrubbed speed.

A CME is a discrete ejection of coronal plasma and magnetic field - distinct from the steady ambient solar wind. NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center publishes CME speeds from slower than 250 km/s to nearly 3000 km/s. The fastest Earth-directed events can reach Earth in as little as 15-18 hours; slower and typical events take several days, commonly about 1-3 days. NASA GSFC overviews put the mass scale at billions of tons (~1e16 g class).

  • Scrub CME speed across the published NOAA SWPC range
  • Play, pause, reset, or drag progress from the Sun toward Earth
  • Read travel-time estimates that use the real 1 AU distance (149,600,000 km)
  • Compare with sibling pages for a historical storm and for ambient solar wind
  • Drag to orbit the camera, scroll or pinch to zoom
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Space-weather teachers use the speed scrub to show why a fast Earth-directed CME shortens warning time. Readers who already know solar wind structure use this page for the discrete ejection and arrival clock that ambient-wind explorers do not teach.

For one famous historical geomagnetic storm, open the Carrington Event 3D Explorer. For the ambient wind bubble around the Sun, open the Solar Wind Heliosphere 3D Explorer.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

This is an educational approximation - on-screen Sun-Earth distance is compressed for readability, while travel time uses the real 1 AU distance divided by the scrubbed speed. It is not an MHD solar-wind model.
