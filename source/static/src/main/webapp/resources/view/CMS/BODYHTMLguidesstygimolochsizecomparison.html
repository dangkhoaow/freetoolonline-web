How the Stygimoloch size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Stygimoloch model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio used by the viewer (3 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker. Other summaries that cite about 3-4.2 m stay disclosed in the facts panel.


What a person would see beside a Stygimoloch

On Stygimoloch Size Comparison, What a person would see is a Late Cretaceous dome-headed biped - turn on the person in the viewer and orbit beside the hip or skull dome to feel how the spikes sit relative to the scale figure. What the Stygimoloch size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a claim that Stygimoloch is proven distinct from adult Pachycephalosaurus: the size comparison discloses about 3 m / 90-450 kg figures and uses 3 m for the Size vs human scale; it does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, and this is not a win/lose game.
