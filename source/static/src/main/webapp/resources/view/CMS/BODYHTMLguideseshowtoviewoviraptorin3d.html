Como ver Oviraptor en 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Revisado por el equipo editorial de FreetoolOnline

Oviraptor 3D Viewer runs a Late Cretaceous Gobi Desert oviraptorid in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the beaked head, body, arms, or legs for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Oviraptor page and take the camera

Open the Oviraptor 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. Orbit with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen keeps the controls visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean glTF may swap in from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural silhouette.


Use the four Oviraptor controls under the canvas

Under the Oviraptor canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (2 m)

Read the Oviraptor facts panel and click a body part

The Oviraptor facts panel lists length about 2 m (published range about 1.6-2 m), height about 0.6 m, weight about 25 kg (published range about 20-40 kg), era 75-71 Mya Late Cretaceous Campanian Djadochta Formation Gobi Desert Mongolia, omnivore/uncertain diet with a toothless beak capable of crushing hard material, named by Henry Fairfield Osborn in 1924 from holotype AMNH 6517 found in 1923 on the Roy Chapman Andrews AMNH Central Asiatic Expeditions. The genus name means egg seizer, but later finds of matching oviraptorid embryos in the same egg type show Oviraptor was very likely brooding its own eggs, not stealing - the name survives for taxonomic priority only. Toothless parrot-like beak; no enlarged sickle foot claw unlike Velociraptor. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. Optional CC0/Public Domain glTF (Model Magic via Printables) may swap in. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Oviraptor how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not claim Oviraptor stole eggs, it does not claim a sickle claw like Velociraptor, and it does not invent game score or win states.
