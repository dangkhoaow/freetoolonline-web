How the Avaceratops size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Avaceratops model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true referred-adult length ratio used by the viewer (4.2 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker. The holotype about 2.3 m stays disclosed in the facts panel and does not claim a full-grown adult at that length.


What a person would see beside an Avaceratops

On Avaceratops Size Comparison, What a person would see is a Late Campanian Montana ceratopsid with a solid frill - turn on the person in the viewer and orbit beside the hip or frill to feel how the smaller ceratopsid sits relative to the scale figure. A license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in after first paint; soft-tissue color remains an artistic choice if the model load fails. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. For larger ceratopsids see the Triceratops 3D Viewer and the Albertaceratops 3D Viewer.


What the Avaceratops size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a claim that the holotype was a full-grown adult at 2.3 m: the size comparison discloses about 2.3-4.2 m / about 1 tonne figures and uses 4.2 m for the Size vs human scale; it does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, and this is not a win/lose game.
