Frequently Asked Questions

When and where did Curiosity land?

Curiosity landed at Bradbury Landing inside Gale Crater, a crater about 155 km across, on 2012-08-06.

How far has Curiosity driven?

About 36.1 km total since landing, as most recently reported by NASA JPL.

How tall is Mount Sharp compared to the crater floor?

Aeolis Mons, informally called Mount Sharp, rises about 5.5 km above the crater floor.

What did Curiosity find on Vera Rubin Ridge?

On Sol 1,750 (2017-07-09) its onboard spectrometers confirmed hematite, an iron-oxide mineral first spotted from orbit.

Where is Curiosity now?

As of the most recently reported update (Nov 2025), it is on Mount Sharp's lower slopes near Gediz Vallis Ridge, in "boxwork"-textured terrain around 4.6°S, 137.4°E. This scene shows discrete published milestones, not a live position feed.

Is the on-screen crater and mountain to scale?

No - the crater, mountain, and route are procedural shapes for teaching purposes, not a terrain-accurate reconstruction of Gale Crater. Only the sol numbers, dates, and distances cited in the facts panel are real published figures.

How is this different from the Mars Terrain explorer?

The Mars Terrain 3D Explorer is a static, sitewide relief map of all of Mars built from real NASA elevation data. This page instead replays one real rover's mission-milestone sequence inside one crater.

Where do these figures come from?

NASA JPL's Mars Science Laboratory mission pages and NASA Science's published mission maps and progress reports.
