Most readers land here holding a number and a question: is this a length of time or a point in time? A three or four digit value is almost always a duration - a timer, a delay, an animation - measured in thousandths of a second. A 13-digit value is almost always a moment: the milliseconds elapsed since 1970-01-01T00:00:00 UTC.

Once you know which one you have, the next step is quick. For a duration, multiply or divide by 1000 to move between seconds and milliseconds. For a moment, read the live value with the time in milliseconds tool, then turn it into a readable date with milliseconds to date. Matching the unit your destination expects is what keeps the result correct.
