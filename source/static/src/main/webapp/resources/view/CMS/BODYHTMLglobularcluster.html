About this scene

The Milky Way hosts about 150 known globular star swarms (up to about 200 estimated behind the disk). They are among the oldest stellar systems, about 11-13 billion years old, span roughly 50-450 light-years, and hold about 10,000 to ~10 million stars (Omega Centauri near the top end).
