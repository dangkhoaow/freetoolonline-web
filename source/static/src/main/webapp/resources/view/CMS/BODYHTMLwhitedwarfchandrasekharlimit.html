About this scene

The facts panel fills when the scene loads. Scrub mass toward the limit.

A white dwarf is supported by electron degeneracy pressure. The classical Chandrasekhar limit is about 1.44 solar masses (NASA Chandra notes often quote about 1.4). Near that ceiling a white dwarf is roughly Earth-sized (~0.01 solar radii) while holding about a Sun's worth of mass, giving densities on the order of ~1 tonne per cubic centimetre.
