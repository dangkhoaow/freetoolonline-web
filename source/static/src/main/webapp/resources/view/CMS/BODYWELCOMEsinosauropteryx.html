Sinosauropteryx 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

This page renders Sinosauropteryx prima as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, the body, the tail, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

The first fossil was found in August 1996 in Liaoning Province, China, and the species was named Sinosauropteryx prima later that year by Chinese paleontologists Ji Qiang and Ji Shu'an. It became famous almost immediately: it was the first non-avian dinosaur ever confirmed to carry feather-like filaments (simple, hair-like protofeathers rather than the branched, flight-capable feathers of birds), a discovery that reshaped how paleontologists pictured dinosaurs. Its fossils come from the Yixian Formation, dated to the Early Cretaceous, about 125 million years ago.

Sinosauropteryx is also one of only a handful of dinosaurs whose original color has been read directly from the fossil. Scientists examined the fossilized melanosomes (pigment-bearing structures) preserved in its filaments and found evidence of a reddish-brown (chestnut) body with a banded, ringed tail - a genuine countershading pattern, not a guess. One specimen even preserved a small lizard and mammal remains inside its stomach, making it the only dinosaur fossil known to contain mammal remains. Its tail is unusually long for its body: about 64 vertebrae, more than half of the animal's total length.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern shown here follow the published melanosome color evidence as closely as a simple 3D model can, but the exact feather texture and fine detail are still an artistic reconstruction - this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
