When to Use Orbital Resonance 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Open the Orbital Resonance 3D Explorer when you need the Io:Europa:Ganymede Laplace beat - periods about 1:2:4 and mean motions about 4:2:1 - plus Neptune:Pluto 3:2 literacy, without a full N-body ephemeris.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Orbital Resonance 3D Explorer, press Play orbits, and watch the white flash when the three moons nearly align - clearer than counting laps on a crowded planet tour alone.


Period and mean-motion literacy in one panel

Period and mean-motion literacy stays readable when you keep the facts table visible while reading periods about 1:2:4, mean motions about 4:2:1, and the Neptune:Pluto 3:2 button as a second classic pair. Circular coplanar rings keep the beat visible without forced-eccentricity integration.


Sessions Orbital Resonance 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Orbital Resonance 3D Explorer does not fit are full planet tours, ellipse-law scrubbers, or banded Jupiter cloud demos. Use Solar System 3D Explorer, Kepler Orbits 3D Explorer, or Gas Giant Atmosphere 3D Explorer instead. The page needs WebGL and is teaching geometry, not a precise conjunction ephemeris.
