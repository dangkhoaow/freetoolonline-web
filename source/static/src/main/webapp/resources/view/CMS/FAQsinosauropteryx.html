Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Sinosauropteryx famous?

It was the first non-avian dinosaur ever confirmed to carry feather-like filaments, discovered in 1996. It is also one of the very few dinosaurs whose original color has been read directly from fossil evidence, rather than guessed.

Do we actually know what color it was?

For the tail and body, yes, unusually. Scientists studied the fossilized melanosomes preserved in its filaments and found evidence of a reddish-brown (chestnut) body with a banded, ringed tail. Fine feather texture and small details are still an artistic reconstruction.

How big was Sinosauropteryx?

The largest known specimen measures about 1.07 m long and roughly 0.55 kg, though more than half of that length is its unusually long tail (about 64 vertebrae). Turn on the human figure in the viewer to compare scale against a 1.8 m person.

What did it eat?

It was a small carnivore. One fossil specimen preserved a small lizard and mammal remains inside its stomach, making Sinosauropteryx the only dinosaur fossil known to contain mammal remains.

When and where was it found?

The first fossil was found in August 1996 in the Yixian Formation, Liaoning Province, China, and named Sinosauropteryx prima later that year by Chinese paleontologists Ji Qiang and Ji Shu'an. The rock layer dates to the Early Cretaceous, about 125 million years ago.

Do I need to install anything to view it?
