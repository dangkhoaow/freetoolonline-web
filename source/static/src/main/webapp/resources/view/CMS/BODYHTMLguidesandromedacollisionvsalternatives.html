Where Andromeda Collision wins

Andromeda Collision wins when the question is "what happens when two real galaxies actually meet" - every milestone in its panel is a published figure for our own galaxy's real future, not a generic teaching example.


Where Spiral Galaxy wins

Spiral Galaxy wins when the question is about one galaxy's own shape - regenerating the spiral with a new arm count makes the parametric nature of spiral structure itself the lesson, independent of any merger.


A reasonable rule

As a reasonable rule, use Spiral Galaxy first to build intuition for what a galaxy's own structure looks like, then use Andromeda Collision to see two of those structures collide and merge on a real timeline.
