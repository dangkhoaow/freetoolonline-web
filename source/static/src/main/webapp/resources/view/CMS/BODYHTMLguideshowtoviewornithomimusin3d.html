How to View Ornithomimus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Ornithomimus 3D Viewer runs a Late Cretaceous Alberta ostrich-mimic - length about 3.3-3.8 m (Size vs human uses 3.8 m), mass about 105-170 kg - smaller than Gallimimus. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body part for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Ornithomimus page and take the camera

Open the Ornithomimus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Ornithomimus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural ostrich-mimic silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Ornithomimus controls under the canvas

Under the Ornithomimus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle breathing and leg sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest length ratio used by the viewer (3.8 m representative within the published about 3.3-3.8 m range; Paul 2010 cites 3.8 m for O. edmontonicus)

Read the Ornithomimus facts panel and click a body part

The Ornithomimus facts panel lists length about 3.3-3.8 m (Paul 2010 about 3.8 m for O. edmontonicus; Size vs human uses 3.8 m), hip height about 1.5 m for a large adult, mass about 105-170 kg (Paul 2010 about 170 kg), the Late Cretaceous window about 76.5-69.6 million years ago in Alberta (Dinosaur Park / Horseshoe Canyon), unresolved diet with omnivory often proposed, naming from Marsh 1890 (O. velox) and Sternberg 1933 (O. edmontonicus), and documented feather fossils from Ornithomimus itself - distinct from the larger Mongolian Gallimimus. A short click - not a drag - on head, body, leg, or neck surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction; feather outline beyond preserved traces is interpretive. When the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Ornithomimus how-to guide is not

The Ornithomimus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the disclosed about 3.3-3.8 m / 105-170 kg ranges, does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, does not invent game score or win states, and does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
