Psittacosaurus 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

This page renders Psittacosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the beak, a forelimb, or a hind leg to read a fact about that part.

Psittacosaurus was an early ceratopsian from Early Cretaceous Asia. Henry Fairfield Osborn named the genus in 1923. Published length estimates commonly fall around 1.9 to 2.5 m and mass around 9 to 20 kg; this viewer uses about 2 m and about 20 kg as mid-range values within those ranges. Unlike Triceratops, it lacked a large frill and horns, and many reconstructions show it as a small biped.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and pattern are an artistic reconstruction, because fossils do not preserve color or soft tissue; this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. Some fossils preserve bristle-like tail integument, which this stylized viewer does not model strand by strand.
