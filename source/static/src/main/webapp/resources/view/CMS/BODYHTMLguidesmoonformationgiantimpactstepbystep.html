How to Explore Moon Formation Giant Impact 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Moon Formation Giant Impact 3D Explorer, step through the five stages from before impact to present-day orbit, and click each body for its facts.

  1. Open /space-3d/moon-formation-giant-impact.html and wait for proto-Earth and Theia to paint at stage 0.
  2. Click Theia (the smaller, reddish sphere) - a Mars-sized protoplanet holding about 10% of Earth's mass in the canonical model.
  3. Drag the stage slider to 1 (Impact) and see Theia close in at an oblique angle of roughly 45 degrees.
  4. Drag the stage slider to 2 (Debris disk) and watch the ejected-material ring appear around Earth.
  5. Drag the stage slider to 4 (Present-day orbit) and click the Moon for its current distance and size.
