Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Monolophosaurus?

The commonly cited estimate is about 5 to 5.5 m long and around 475 kg. A few older popular sources give much larger figures, up to about 7.5 m, but the 1993 scientific description does not support those numbers. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

What was the crest on its head for?

The single crest runs along the midline of the snout and is built from the fused nasal and lacrimal bones. It is hollow inside and connects to the antorbital fossa (the opening in front of the eye) through small internal passages. Researchers think it more likely served display or species recognition than a weapon, since it is thin and not well suited to fighting.

How is Monolophosaurus different from Dilophosaurus or Cryolophosaurus?

All three are crested Jurassic theropods, but the crests differ: Monolophosaurus carries a single fused ridge along the snout midline, Dilophosaurus (North America) carries a pair of parallel crests, and Cryolophosaurus (Antarctica) carries a single crest that sweeps forward like a pompadour. Monolophosaurus is also the only one of the three known from China.

When and where did Monolophosaurus live?

In the Middle Jurassic, about 163 to 161 million years ago, in the Shishugou Formation of the Junggar Basin, Xinjiang, China. Dong Zhiming found the skeleton in 1981 while surveying rock layers for the oil industry, and Zhao Xijin and Philip Currie formally named it Monolophosaurus jiangi in 1993.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, skull length, and age figures shown are real published values.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
