When the Giganotosaurus browser viewer fits

Pick the Giganotosaurus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on one of the largest predatory dinosaurs known, Size vs human at the honest about 12.5 m length ratio inside the disclosed 12-13 m (up to about 13.2 m) range, and click facts on head, arm, tail, or leg - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of Candeleros Formation numbers (99.6-95 Mya; Ruben Dario Carolini near Villa El Chocon in 1993; Rodolfo Coria and Leonardo Salgado 1995; holotype MUCPv-Ch1 at the Ernesto Bachmann Municipal Paleontological Museum).


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted Giganotosaurus bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match - including the Ernesto Bachmann Municipal Paleontological Museum in Villa El Chocon for the holotype context. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Giganotosaurus comparison is not

This Giganotosaurus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise weight (the wide 4.2-13.8 tonne range is intentional), it does not claim Giganotosaurus was definitively larger or smaller than Tyrannosaurus rex, it does not invent a specific proven prey list, it does not invent a glTF credit, and it does not invent game score or win states.
