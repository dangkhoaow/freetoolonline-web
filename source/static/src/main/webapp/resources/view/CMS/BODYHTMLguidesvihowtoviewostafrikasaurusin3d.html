Use the four Ostafrikasaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Ostafrikasaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (8 m (speculative scale only))

Read the Ostafrikasaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Ostafrikasaurus facts panel lists holotype tooth about 46 mm (Buffetaut 2012) as the securely published measurement. Body length and mass are not known from fossils - this tooth-only taxon has no postcranial bones. The scene uses length 8 m and weight 1,150 kg only as a speculative reconstruction for the Size vs human toggle - never as measured values. Era Late Jurassic Tendaguru Formation Tanzania (exact stage debated), carnivore diet inferred from serrated spinosaurid-type tooth morphology, named by Eric Buffetaut in 2012 as Ostafrikasaurus crassiserratus. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. Optional CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF (seth the yutyrannus via Printables 1023409) may swap in. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Ostafrikasaurus how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not claim a measured body length or mass from fossils, it labels the full body as speculative because only a tooth is known, and it does not invent game score or win states.
