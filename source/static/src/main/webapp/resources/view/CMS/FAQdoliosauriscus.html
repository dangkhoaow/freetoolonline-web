Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Doliosauriscus?

Published summaries give a skull about 53 cm long and a total body length of about 3-4 m. This page uses 3.5 m as the facts-panel length baseline. Mass is not tightly constrained in the primary literature; the panel shows about 500 kg as a proportionate reconstruction baseline and notes that limit. Turn on the human figure to compare with a 1.8 m person.

Was Doliosauriscus a dinosaur?

No. It was an anteosaurid dinocephalian synapsid - on the mammal line, not the dinosaur line. It lived in the Middle Permian of Russia, tens of millions of years before the first dinosaurs. The familiar name Doliosauriscus is often synonymized with Titanophoneus in modern taxonomy; this page keeps Doliosauriscus and states that synonymy.

When and where did Doliosauriscus live?

In Middle Permian rocks of Russia, about 268-260 million years ago (Guadalupian / Capitanian interval associated with the Isheevo-style anteosaur faunas). Orlov described the animal as Doliosaurus in 1958; Kuhn renamed it Doliosauriscus in 1961.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published size figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, skull, and age figures shown are real published values; mass is presented with an honesty note because a tight published consensus mass was not found.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
