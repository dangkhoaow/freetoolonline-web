When to Use Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Open the Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer when you need a classroom or curiosity pass over transit depth from (Rplanet/Rstar) squared - Jupiter-Sun about 1% versus Earth-Sun about 0.0084% - without pretending the scene is a photometry pipeline.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer, select Jupiter depth, press Play orbit, and pause when the star dims about 1%. Switch to Earth depth and read 0.0084% in the panel - faster than a static light-curve sketch alone.


TRAPPIST-1 and multi-planet context

TRAPPIST-1 and multi-planet context stay readable when you toggle Show TRAPPIST-1 note while scrubbing - 7 transiting planets with 1.5 to 19 day periods from the published catalog. The depth follows (Rplanet/Rstar) squared in the panel; the scene does not add photon noise or limb darkening.


Sessions Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer does not fit are solar-system planet tours only, stellar evolution timelines, radial-velocity or microlensing methods, or real telescope FITS analysis. Use Solar System 3D Explorer or Star Lifecycle 3D Explorer instead. The transit page needs WebGL and is not matched to a live observation schedule.
