Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Smilodon?

About 2.5 m long and 1 m tall at the shoulder, weighing near 280 kg - close to a modern lion but more heavily built. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

How long were its sabre teeth?

Its upper canine teeth grew up to about 28 cm long. They likely delivered a precise killing bite rather than being used to fight through bone, since they were too thin to survive heavy impact.

Was Smilodon a tiger?

No. Despite the popular nickname "sabre-tooth tiger," Smilodon was not closely related to tigers or any living big cat - it belonged to its own extinct branch of the cat family.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow the fossil skeleton, but the fur color and pattern are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. The length, weight, and canine-length figures shown are real published values.

When and where did Smilodon live?

From about 2.5 million years ago until roughly 10,000 years ago, across the Americas. Thousands of skeletons have been recovered from the La Brea Tar Pits in Los Angeles.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
