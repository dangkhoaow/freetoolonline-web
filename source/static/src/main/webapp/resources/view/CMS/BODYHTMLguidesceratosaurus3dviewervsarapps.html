When the Ceratosaurus browser viewer fits

Pick the Ceratosaurus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a nasal-horn theropod with Size vs human at the honest 6 m length ratio, and click facts on head, body, neck, tail, or leg - without installing anything. Nothing about the visit is sent to a server - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the published figures and the optional CC-BY-SA 4.0 model credit.


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside Morrison theropod mounts and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Ceratosaurus comparison is not

This Ceratosaurus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands, it does not invent AR features the page does not have, it does not invent one precise length or weight beyond the disclosed about 5.3-7 m / 0.4-0.7 t figures, it does not claim the nasal horn was a combat weapon, it does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction, and it does not invent game score or win states.
