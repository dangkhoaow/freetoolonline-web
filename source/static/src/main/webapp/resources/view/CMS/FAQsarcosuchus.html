Frequently Asked Questions

Was Sarcosuchus a dinosaur?

No. Sarcosuchus was a crocodyliform - a distant relative of modern crocodiles - that lived alongside dinosaurs but belongs to a separate group. This viewer includes it because it is one of the most-searched giant prehistoric predators.

How big was Sarcosuchus?

Current published estimates put it around 9.5 m long and 3.45-4.3 t; an older estimate, based on the single largest known skull, ran as high as 11.65 m and about 8 t. This page uses the current, more conservative figures as its panel baseline. Turn on the human figure to compare with a person.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published fossil reconstructions - a low sprawled body, a long tapered snout, and a dorsal armor ridge - but the skin color and pattern are an artistic reconstruction. Fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The figures shown are real published values.

Where and when was Sarcosuchus found?

Fossils were recovered between 1946 and 1959 by Albert-Felix de Lapparent in what is now Niger and Brazil. Paul Sereno described more complete specimens in 1997 and 2000, which is when the "SuperCroc" nickname became widely known. It lived in the Early Cretaceous, roughly 112-97 million years ago.

How is Sarcosuchus different from a modern crocodile?

It was several times longer and heavier than any living crocodile, and it belongs to a different, extinct branch of the crocodyliform family tree rather than being a direct ancestor of today's crocodiles or alligators.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
