Frequently Asked Questions

How big was a real Oviraptor?

About 2 m long (published ranges run 1.6-2 m) and roughly 20 to 40 kg - turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the true scale against a 1.8 m person.

Did Oviraptor really steal eggs?

No. The name means "egg seizer" because the 1923 type specimen was found near a nest then attributed to Protoceratops. Later fossil finds of matching embryos inside the same egg type showed Oviraptor was very likely brooding its own eggs, not raiding a nest. The name stuck anyway, for taxonomic priority.

Did Oviraptor have teeth?

No. It had a toothless, curved, parrot-like beak likely strong enough to crush hard food. This model shows the beak and skips teeth entirely, matching the fossil skull.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow the fossil skeleton, but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values. Unlike dromaeosaurids such as Velociraptor, Oviraptor did not have an enlarged sickle-shaped foot claw, so this model does not add one.

When and where did Oviraptor live?

In the Late Cretaceous, roughly 75 to 71 million years ago, in the Djadochta Formation of what is now the Gobi Desert of Mongolia. The holotype was found in 1923 and the genus was named in 1924.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
