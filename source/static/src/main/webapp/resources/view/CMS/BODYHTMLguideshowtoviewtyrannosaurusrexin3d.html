How to View Tyrannosaurus rex in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Tyrannosaurus rex 3D Viewer runs a reconstructive model in your browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click the head, tail, or a leg to read a part fact.


Open the Tyrannosaurus rex page and take the camera

Open the Tyrannosaurus rex 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit the Tyrannosaurus rex with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene.


Use the four Tyrannosaurus rex controls under the canvas

Under the Tyrannosaurus rex canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation - freezes auto-spin (the label flips to Auto-rotate when spinning is off)
  • Idle motion - adds a subtle hind-leg sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person scaled to the real ~12.3 m length so the height comparison stays honest

Read the Tyrannosaurus rex facts panel and click body parts

The Tyrannosaurus rex facts panel lists length (~12.3 m), weight (~8.9 tonnes), the 68-66 Mya Late Cretaceous window, diet (carnivore), and the 1902 Montana description by Barnum Brown. A short click - not a drag - on the head, tail, or a leg swaps in a part note; click empty space to return to the species sheet. Skin color is an artistic reconstruction; the figures are the published ones.


What the Tyrannosaurus rex how-to guide is not

The Tyrannosaurus rex how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR) and it does not claim soft-tissue accuracy.
