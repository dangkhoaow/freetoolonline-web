Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Mapusaurus?

Published length estimates range from about 10.2 m, based on the largest femur described in the original 2006 paper, to about 12.6 m in later studies. Weight estimates run from about 6 to 8 tonnes. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

Did Mapusaurus hunt in packs?

A bonebed of seven to nine individuals was found together at Canadon del Gato in Argentina, which has led some researchers to suggest Mapusaurus may have hunted in groups, similar to its close relative Giganotosaurus. This remains debated - a bonebed shows several animals died in the same place, but it does not by itself prove they hunted together while alive.

When and where did Mapusaurus live?

In the Late Cretaceous, roughly 97 to 93.5 million years ago (the Cenomanian to Turonian), in what is now the Huincul Formation of Neuquen Province, Argentina. The Argentine-Canadian Dinosaur Project excavated the bonebed between 1997 and 2001, and Rodolfo Coria and Philip J. Currie formally described and named the species in 2006.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and texture are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited because sources disagree.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
