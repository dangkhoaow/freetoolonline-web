Spinosaurus Size Comparison

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Spinosaurus 3D Viewer pairs published sail-backed figures with a 1.8 m person on screen - length about 14 m, hip height about 2.1 m, and weight about 7.4 tonnes (7400 kg) - so the theropod scale gap stays readable at a glance.

How the Spinosaurus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Spinosaurus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length-to-hip-height ratio for the about 14 m figure (hip height about 2.1 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Spinosaurus

At about 2.1 m at the hip, a Spinosaurus hip line sits a little above a 1.8 m person's head. From snout to tail the animal spans about 14 m - turn on the person in the viewer and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how the long snout and dorsal sail tower past the scale figure.


What the Spinosaurus size numbers are not

The Spinosaurus size comparison uses published estimates with an honest length range; skin and sail color are an artistic reconstruction, and a fully aquatic lifestyle is not settled science on the viewer page.
