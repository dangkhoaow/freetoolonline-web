Remove Audio from Video

Last reviewed: 2026-07-13

Strip the audio track from a video file, right in the browser.

The output keeps the original video quality, resolution, and container format; only the audio track is dropped.

The file is processed in this browser tab and is never uploaded to a server. Nothing is installed and no account is needed.

This removes the entire audio track - it does not selectively mute or duck one section. The input must be a video file; audio-only files such as MP3 or WAV are rejected with a clear error, since there is no video track to keep.

This will not work on a DRM-protected or encrypted video stream; if the file plays only inside a locked-down app or store, expect the tool to fail on it rather than silently strip a track it cannot read.

To trim or cut the video instead of removing its audio, see the related-tools section below.
