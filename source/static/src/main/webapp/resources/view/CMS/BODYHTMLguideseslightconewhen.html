When to Use Light Cone 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

Open the Light Cone 3D Explorer when you need Minkowski causal structure - c=299,792.458 km/s, 45 deg null lines - and skip it when you only need gravity wells or spectral redshift.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Light Cone 3D Explorer, drag the probe event into the elsewhere region, and pause so students see the causal label flip while the green future cone and orange past cone stay fixed at 45 deg.


Causal-structure literacy in Light Cone 3D Explorer

Causal-structure literacy in Light Cone 3D Explorer stays readable when you keep the facts panel open for c=299,792.458 km/s and the 45 deg null-line convention while snapping to a null ray.


Sessions Light Cone 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Light Cone 3D Explorer does not fit are curved-spacetime GR solving, geodesic integration, or spectral redshift modeling. Use Gravity Well 3D or Redshift Doppler 3D instead. Walk the controls in the Light Cone 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
