When to Use Apollo Free-Return Trajectory 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Open the Apollo Free-Return Trajectory 3D Explorer when you need a lunar free-return vs hybrid path comparison - without a patched-conic mission designer.


A crew-safety trajectory lesson

A crew-safety lesson starts when you open the explorer on Free-return (early design PC ~110 km), scrub to the lunar flyby, then switch to Hybrid to show why Apollo 13 needed the 34.23 s DPS-1 burn at GET 61:29:43 after the oxygen-tank failure.


Apollo 13 distance and pericynthion figures

Apollo 13 distance and pericynthion figures land cleanly here: 400,171 km furthest from Earth at PC, ~250 km restored free-return lunar altitude, and the ~2,500 nmi hybrid Earth perigee that would not capture.


Sessions this explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are two-burn circular-orbit transfers or full mission design. Use the Hohmann Transfer 3D Explorer for delta-v ellipses between circular orbits. Walk the controls in the step-by-step guide.
