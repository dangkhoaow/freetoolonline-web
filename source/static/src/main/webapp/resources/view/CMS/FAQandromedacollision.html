Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Milky Way - Andromeda Collision 3D Explorer show?

The real, published timeline for the eventual merger of the Milky Way and the Andromeda Galaxy (M31). Scrub the Time slider from today out to about 10 billion years, or press Play, to watch the two galaxies approach, stretch into tidal tails, and merge.

Will the Milky Way really collide with Andromeda?

The classic Hubble-based scenario says yes, with a first close pass in about 3.75-4 billion years and a full merger by about 7 billion years. A 2025 study lowered the merger probability to about 50% using then-current data; a 2026 recalibration using improved Gaia data raised it back to about 90%, largely re-confirming the classic outcome.

How is this different from the Spiral Galaxy 3D Explorer?

Spiral Galaxy 3D Explorer renders one galaxy's shape in isolation. This page renders two real galaxies - the Milky Way and Andromeda - approaching and merging over billions of years on the real published timeline.

Why do the galaxies stretch into long tails?

Tidal tails form when each galaxy's gravity pulls stars out of the other's disk during a close pass - the same distortion astronomers observe today in real merging pairs like the Antennae Galaxies. The tails appear here once the timeline passes the first close pass at about 3.75 billion years.

Is this a live simulation of real gravity?

No. It is an educational approximation tuned to teach the sequence of a galaxy merger, not a gravitational simulation. The separation, approach speed, and Gyr milestones shown in the facts panel are the real published figures; the disk shapes and tail stretching are illustrative.

Will anyone be around to see this happen?

No. The first close pass is roughly 3.75-4 billion years away and the full merger roughly 7 billion years away - both far beyond any human timescale, and even beyond the Sun's own expected lifetime as a normal star.

Are the galaxies drawn to their real scale and distance?

No. Both galaxies are drawn far larger relative to their separation than their true scale, so the merger stays visible on screen - disclosed in the facts panel and the page text above.
