How the Troodon size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Troodon model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 2.4 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Troodon

What a person would see is a chicken-sized theropod whose panel length is 2.4 m - shorter than a 1.8 m person end-to-end - with hip height about 0.6 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how a small Late Cretaceous predator compares to you.


What the Troodon size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a blended composite: the panel headline figures (2.4 m / 0.6 m hip / 50 kg) are never treated as if every published estimate matched the panel baseline. Feather color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. The genus validity debate is disclosed separately. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
