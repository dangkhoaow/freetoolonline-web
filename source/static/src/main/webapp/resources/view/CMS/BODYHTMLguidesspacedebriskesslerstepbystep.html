How to Explore Space Debris & Kessler Syndrome 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open Space Debris & Kessler Syndrome 3D Explorer, scrub the year slider from 2007 to 2026, and watch tracked debris and satellites grow across four altitude bands.

  1. Open /space-3d/space-debris-kessler.html and wait for the Earth sphere and four altitude-band point clouds to render.
  2. Drag the year slider from 2007 toward 2026 and watch the dot density grow in every band; the facts panel updates the tracked-object count as you move it.
  3. Click "Highlight 550km Starlink shell" to see the 500-600km mega-constellation band called out in bright green against the other three bands.
  4. Click "2007: Fengyun-1C test" to jump to that year and read about the real anti-satellite test that created more than 3,500 tracked fragments.
  5. Click "2009: Iridium-Cosmos collision" to jump to that year and read the reference accidental-collision case.
  6. Click "Reset to 2026" to return to the current-day view.
