When to Use Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

Open the Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer when you need the perspective-geometry picture behind shower streaks - Perseids peak Aug 12-13 at ZHR ~100, parent 109P/Swift-Tuttle - and skip it when you need a live observing forecast or a planetary scene.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer, toggle from Perseids to Leonids, and pause so students see the streaks keep pointing back to the same radiant marker while the parent-comet label changes to 55P/Tempel-Tuttle.


Radiant literacy in Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer

Radiant literacy in Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer stays readable when you keep the facts panel open for Aug 12-13 / ZHR ~100 / Swift-Tuttle while scrubbing trail length and speed.


Sessions Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer does not fit are live cloud-cover or moonlight forecasts, local visibility windows, or real meteoroid orbit integration. Use a live observing app for tonight's forecast instead. Walk the controls in the Meteor Shower Radiant 3D Explorer step-by-step guide.
