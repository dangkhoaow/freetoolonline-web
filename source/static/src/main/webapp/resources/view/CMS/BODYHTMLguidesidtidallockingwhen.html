When to Use Tidal Locking 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-13

The Tidal Locking 3D Explorer fits classroom demos of why one lunar face stays Earthward, short curiosity breaks, and literacy around the 27.32-day spin-orbit match. It is an educational geometry visualization - not a tidal-friction solver - so a few session types need another tool.


A five-minute classroom demo

Open Tidal Locking 3D Explorer in Tidally locked mode, press Play orbit, then switch Unlocked spin so students see the far side appear - faster than a chalkboard sketch alone.


Recession and day-lengthening literacy

Pause and read 3.8 cm/yr lunar recession and 1.7 ms/century day lengthening in the panel table - a bridge from geometry to published tidal rates.


Sessions it does not fit

These sessions do not fit Tidal Locking 3D Explorer: any time you need Moon phase lit fraction, exoplanet transit depth, or lunar libration wobble as the main lesson. Use Moon Phases 3D Explorer, Exoplanet Transit 3D Explorer, or Moon Calendar 3D Explorer instead. It also needs WebGL in the browser.
