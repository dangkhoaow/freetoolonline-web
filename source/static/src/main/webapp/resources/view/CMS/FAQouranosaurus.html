Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Ouranosaurus?

The Natural History Museum lists length about 7.0 m. Other published estimates reach about 8.3 m, and weight estimates range from about 2.2 to 4 tonnes (Paul 2010 cites about 2.2 tonnes). Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

Was Ouranosaurus related to Spinosaurus?

No. Both animals had a sail of elongated neural spines, but Ouranosaurus was a beaked iguanodontian herbivore from Early Cretaceous Niger. Spinosaurus was a much larger fish-eating theropod from North Africa tens of millions of years later. The sail is a convergent look, not proof they were close relatives.

When and where did Ouranosaurus live?

In the Early Cretaceous, about 115 to 100 million years ago, in what is now Niger. Philippe Taquet named Ouranosaurus nigeriensis in 1976 from fossils found in the Elrhaz Formation in 1965.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values, with ranges cited because sources vary.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. A free-licensed glTF model swaps in after first paint to show the sail-backed body.
