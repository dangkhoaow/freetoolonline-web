Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a real wormhole simulation?

No. It is an educational embedding diagram of the exact catenoid shape general relativity permits for a zero-tidal-force throat. It does not solve the full Einstein field equations and does not model exotic matter.

Have wormholes ever been observed?

No. No wormhole has ever been observed. The geometry is a solution the math of general relativity allows, first found by Ludwig Flamm in 1916.

Can anything actually travel through one?

Not the version shown by default. Wheeler and Fuller showed in 1962 that a Schwarzschild wormhole pinches shut before even light can cross. Morris and Thorne showed in 1988 that a traversable version would need exotic matter that has never been observed.

What does the throat-radius slider do?

It changes b0 in the formula r(l) = b0*cosh(l/b0), rescaling the entire catenoid mesh live so you can see the throat widen or narrow relative to the two flared mouths.

What is the traversal marker?

A small illustrative marker that animates from mouth A to mouth B along the throat surface. It is a visual aid only, not a claim that crossing is physically possible.

Who coined the word "wormhole"?

Charles Misner and John Wheeler, in a 1957 paper.
