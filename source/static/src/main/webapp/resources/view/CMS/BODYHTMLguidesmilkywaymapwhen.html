When to Use Milky Way Map 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Open the Milky Way Map 3D Explorer when you need a labeled top-down map of our galaxy - Sagittarius, Orion (Local), and Perseus arms with the Sun about 26,000 ly from the center on a disk about 100,000 ly across - without a Gaia survey dump.


A five-minute classroom demo

A five-minute classroom demo starts when you open Milky Way Map 3D Explorer, press Sun, then Orion and Perseus so students see labeled arms against the disk - clearer than a flat unlabeled spiral alone.


Galactic year literacy in one panel

In Milky Way Map 3D Explorer, Galactic year literacy stays readable when you keep the facts table visible while reading diameter about 100,000 ly, Sun about 26,000 ly from center, and a galactic year about 230 Myr (range 225-250). Play spin keeps a slow educational beat without claiming N-body dynamics.


Sessions Milky Way Map 3D Explorer does not fit

Sessions Milky Way Map 3D Explorer does not fit are random unlabeled spiral regenerations, planet tours, or Mars retrograde demos. Use Galaxy 3D Simulator for a generic spiral, Solar System 3D Explorer for planets, or Retrograde Motion 3D Explorer for retrograde. The page needs WebGL and is parametric teaching art, not a Gaia catalog.
