Frequently Asked Questions

What does Parallax Distance 3D Explorer show?

Earth at opposite ends of a 1 AU orbit, sight lines to a star, and how changing parallax in arcseconds moves the star with distance d = 1/p in parsecs.

What is the parallax distance formula?

d (pc) = 1 / p (arcsec). A star with parallax 1 arcsec sits at 1 parsec; 0.5 arcsec sits at 2 pc.

How far is one parsec?

About 3.26 light-years, or about 206,265 AU - the distance where Earth's orbit subtends one arcsecond.

What about Proxima Centauri?

Its published parallax is about 0.7687 arcsec, which implies about 1.30 pc (~4.24 ly) with d = 1/p.

How is this different from Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer?

Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer teaches apparent vs absolute brightness. Parallax Distance 3D Explorer isolates the Earth-baseline triangle that measures distance.

Are the on-screen angles true to scale?

No. Real stellar parallaxes are below one arcsecond, so the teaching stage exaggerates the triangle so the geometry stays readable.
