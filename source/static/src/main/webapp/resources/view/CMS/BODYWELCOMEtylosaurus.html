Last reviewed: 2026-07-18

This page renders a Tylosaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the snout, a flipper, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Tylosaurus was a Late Cretaceous mosasaur, about 90 to 66 million years ago, from the North American Western Interior Seaway - not a dinosaur. Large adults are often cited near 8-14 m (some specimens near 12-13.2 m); mass estimates span about 1.1 tonnes to several tonnes. It is distinct from Mosasaurus, the European form often reconstructed longer near 11-17 m.
