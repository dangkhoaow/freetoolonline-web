Earth's atmosphere has no wall at the edge of space - it thins out gradually across four named layers, and two different agencies draw the "edge of space" line at two different altitudes. Drag the altitude probe from the ground up to 650 km to see which layer you are in and how far you sit from the Karman line, the US boundary, and the ISS.
