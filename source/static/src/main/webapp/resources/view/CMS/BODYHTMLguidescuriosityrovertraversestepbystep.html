How to Explore the Curiosity Rover Traverse 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Curiosity Rover Traverse 3D Explorer and scrub through 6 real NASA mission milestones, from the 2012 touchdown to the rover's most recently reported position on Mount Sharp.

  1. Open /space-3d/curiosity-rover-traverse.html and wait for the crater floor, Mount Sharp, the route line, and the rover marker to render.
  2. Drag the milestone slider (0-5) to move the rover to each of the 6 real published mission milestones.
  3. Read the facts panel for that milestone's sol number, date, name, and (where officially reported) distance driven.
  4. Check the running table below the facts panel - the highlighted row is the active milestone.
  5. Drag to orbit and scroll or pinch to zoom on the scene for a closer look at the route.
  6. Compare the Pahrump Hills and Gediz Vallis Ridge area milestones to see how far the rover has driven since landing.
