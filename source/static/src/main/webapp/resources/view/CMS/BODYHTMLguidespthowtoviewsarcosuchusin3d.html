Como ver Sarcosuchus em 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Revisado pela equipe editorial FreetoolOnline

Sarcosuchus 3D Viewer runs a giant Early Cretaceous crocodyliform nicknamed SuperCroc in your browser - not a dinosaur. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click head, body, or tail for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Sarcosuchus page and take the camera

Open the Sarcosuchus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. Orbit with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen keeps the controls visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean glTF may swap in from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural silhouette.


Use the four Sarcosuchus controls under the canvas

Under the Sarcosuchus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio (9.5 m)

Read the Sarcosuchus facts panel and click a body part

The Sarcosuchus facts panel lists about 9.5 m (current estimate, panel baseline; older estimate up to 11.65 m based on the largest known skull), about 3.45-4.3 t (current estimate, panel baseline 3.9 t; older estimate around 8 t), height about 1.2 m, about 112-97 Mya Early Cretaceous in Niger (Sarcosuchus imperator) and Brazil (S. hartti), carnivore diet, fossils first found 1946-1959 by Albert-Felix de Lapparent with major specimens described by Paul Sereno in 1997 and 2000. Sarcosuchus was a giant prehistoric crocodyliform - not a dinosaur. It walked with a sprawled gait with legs splayed to the side, unlike tucked-under-body theropods. Not related to the Mosasaurus viewer - one is a river crocodyliform, the other an ocean-going marine reptile. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. Optional CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF (seth the yutyrannus via Printables 764457) is stylized, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Sarcosuchus how-to guide is not

This how-to is a controls walkthrough only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not invent a single length or mass outside published ranges, it states Sarcosuchus was not a dinosaur, it does not conflate Sarcosuchus with Mosasaurus or Deinosuchus, and it does not invent game score or win states.
