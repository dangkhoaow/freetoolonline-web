Void Trader vs Other Space Games

Void Trader is a real-time space trading roguelike that runs in the browser with no install (~50 KB, Canvas 2D). Its distinctive loop is mine, trade, upgrade, and clear eight sectors with one life. Here is how it compares with other browser space games on this site and with installed alternatives.

Last reviewed: 2026-07-17

vs Asteroid Blaster (arcade shooter)

Asteroid Blaster is a classic vector-style shooter: rotate, thrust, and break rocks that split into smaller pieces. There is no trading, cargo, or ship upgrades - the whole run is combat and score. Void Trader is about five times smaller (~50 KB vs ~245 KB) and adds a station economy (five goods, Scout to Cruiser upgrades) on top of combat. Pick Asteroid Blaster for pure twitch shooting; pick Void Trader when you want prices and cargo decisions between fights.

vs Gravity Orbit Golf (physics puzzle)

Gravity Orbit Golf is an n-body gravity golf game (~41 KB): fling a comet through procedural solar systems and beat a stroke count. It has no pirates, no trade goods, and no permadeath combat - best strokes save in the browser. Void Trader keeps a live ship under fire across eight sectors with no saves at all. Pick Gravity Orbit Golf for quiet puzzle runs; pick Void Trader for a one-life trading run.

vs installed space-trader apps

Native space-trading games (desktop or mobile stores) often ship large campaigns, 3D art, and online markets, but they need a download and install, often an account, and sometimes purchases. Void Trader trades scope for zero friction: one ~50 KB load in a tab, no account, no localStorage, and no WebGL requirement. It plays best with a keyboard and mouse; there are no touch controls.
