Anchiornis 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

This page renders Anchiornis as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, the body, the tail, or a leg to read a fact about that part.

Anchiornis lived in the Late Jurassic, about 160 million years ago, in what is now Liaoning, China, and was named by Xu Xing and colleagues in 2009. It is one of the oldest known feathered dinosaurs and a four-winged paravian - long, pennaceous feathers grew on both the arms and the legs, not just the arms. The holotype specimen measures about 34 cm long and roughly 110 g; the largest known specimens reach around 60 to 62 cm with a wingspan near 57 cm and a mass cited in the range of 0.6 to 1 kg across published individuals.

Anchiornis is also one of the very few dinosaurs whose original color pattern has been read directly from the fossil rather than guessed. Li and colleagues (2010) examined fossilized melanosomes preserved in its feathers and reconstructed a mostly grey-black body with a reddish-brown crown and white wing feathers tipped in black - a real, published color map, not an artist's invention. For a younger four-winged relative from the Early Cretaceous, see the Microraptor 3D Viewer.

Everything runs on your device with WebGL, so the model works without an account and without sending anything to a server. The skin tone and feather pattern shown here follow the published melanosome color reconstruction as closely as a simple 3D model can, but the exact feather texture and fine detail are still an artistic reconstruction - this model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
