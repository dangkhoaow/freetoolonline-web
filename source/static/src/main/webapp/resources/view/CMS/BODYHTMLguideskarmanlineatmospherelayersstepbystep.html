How to Explore Karman Line & Atmosphere Layers 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Karman Line & Atmosphere Layers 3D Explorer, drag the altitude probe from the ground toward 650 km, and jump straight to any of the five real boundary markers.

  1. Open /space-3d/karman-line-atmosphere-layers.html and wait for Earth and the four layer shells to render.
  2. Drag the altitude slider up from 0 km and watch the probe rise through the troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, and thermosphere.
  3. Click "US boundary (80 km)" then "Karman line (100 km)" to see the roughly 20 km gap between the two competing edge-of-space definitions.
  4. Click "ISS orbit (400 km)" to see how far above both boundaries the space station actually orbits.
  5. Toggle any layer or marker checkbox off to see the others more clearly, then turn on "Meteor & aurora zone" to see where those two phenomena happen.
  6. Read the facts panel for the current layer, the two nearest markers, and the full figures table.
