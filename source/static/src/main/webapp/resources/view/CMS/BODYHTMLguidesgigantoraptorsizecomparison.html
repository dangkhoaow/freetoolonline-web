How the Gigantoraptor size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Gigantoraptor model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 8 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Gigantoraptor

What a person would see is a giant beaked oviraptorosaur whose panel length is about 8 m - roughly four body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with height about 3.5 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the snout to feel how a Late Cretaceous giant oviraptorosaur compares to you.


What the Gigantoraptor size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays the disclosed about 1.4-3.5 t range with panel baseline 2 t / 2000 kg.
