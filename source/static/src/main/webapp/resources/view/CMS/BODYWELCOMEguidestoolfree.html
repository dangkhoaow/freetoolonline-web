The "free" claim a reader scans in a search-result listing usually means one of three things, and only one of them matches the way this site works: an ad-supported page that runs the conversion inside the browser tab so the file never leaves the device, with the bandwidth bill paid by AdSense rather than a subscription. The other two free patterns - a free tier behind a signup gate that nudges toward a paid plan, and an open page that watermarks the output unless a paid plan removes the limit - read the same word "free" but cost the reader differently. The reader-test in the body of this guide (does it ask for an email, does it watermark the output, does it POST your file to a server) tells you which pattern you are reading on any tool page across the web.

Free of an account, free of an upload, and free of a watermark is the contract every in-browser tool on this site offers. The contract holds because the work is JavaScript or WebAssembly that loaded with the page; the server's only job is to deliver that script and an ad banner. If the reader prefers to verify the contract themselves rather than trust the claim in this guide, the Network tab in any modern browser is the canonical check - a POST to a backend with the file's bytes is the signature of an upload, and the absence of one is the signature of an in-browser run.
