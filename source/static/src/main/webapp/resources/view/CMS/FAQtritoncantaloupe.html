Frequently Asked Questions

What is cantaloupe terrain?

A Triton-only landscape of closely spaced, melon-like dimples (cavi) about 30-40 kilometers across, mapped by Voyager 2 in 1989 on the western hemisphere.

How did the dimples form?

Leading ideas include diapirism and global cooling contraction. Evidence is debated. This page shows geometry, not a formation verdict.

What do Play, Dimples, and Speed do?

Play starts or pauses spin. Dimples shows or hides the schematic cavi grid. Speed changes how fast the animation runs.

Is this a Voyager terrain model?

No. It is a procedural schematic grounded in published sizes. Dimples are exaggerated for visibility.

Does this show Triton's geysers?

No. Voyager saw nitrogen geysers elsewhere on Triton; this explorer focuses on cantaloupe terrain only.
