Remove Audio From Video When To Use

Last reviewed: 2026-07-13

Remove Audio from Video is worth opening when you need a quick, one-off run without installing anything. This page covers when that fits and when it does not.


When it fits

Remove Audio from Video fits when the whole audio track needs to go and the picture itself should stay untouched - the output keeps the original video quality, resolution, and container format; only the audio track is dropped.

The whole track is removed at once; there is no option to mute or duck just part of the audio and keep the rest.


What to expect

What to expect is a video-only file with no re-encoding of the picture. The file is processed in this browser tab and is never uploaded to a server.

An audio-only file (mp3, wav, aac, ogg, flac, or m4a) has no video track to keep, so it is rejected with a clear error instead of being processed.

To trim or cut the video instead of removing its audio, see the related-tools section. For a closer look at how this compares with other ways to handle a video's audio, see remove audio from video vs alternatives.
