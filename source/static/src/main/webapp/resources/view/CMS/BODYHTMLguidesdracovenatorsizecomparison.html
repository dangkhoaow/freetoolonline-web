How the Dracovenator size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Dracovenator model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 6.0 m panel baseline (within the published about 5.5-6.5 m range). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Dracovenator

What a person would see is a theropod whose panel length is about 6.0 m - roughly 3.3 body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 1.8 m high in the panel. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside a hind limb to feel how the skull-based reconstruction sits next to the scale figure.


What the Dracovenator size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a full-skeleton census: only skull material is known, so body length stays in the published 5.5-6.5 m range (panel 6.0 m).
