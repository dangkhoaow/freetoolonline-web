How to Explore Dwarf Planet Size Comparison 3D Explorer - Step by Step

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

The Dwarf Planet Size Comparison 3D Explorer starts as soon as the page loads: drag to orbit the view, scroll or pinch to zoom, and watch five spheres already lined up left to right by real distance from the Sun. Everything renders on your own device - no install, no account.


Look at the lineup before clicking anything

Look at the lineup before clicking anything - Ceres sits alone near the left edge, small and close, while Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, and Eris sit grouped together farther out, roughly matched in size to each other and much larger than Ceres.


Click a name to highlight it

Click a name to highlight it in the Dwarf Planet Size Comparison 3D visualizer: press Ceres, Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, or Eris and the camera jumps to that sphere while the facts panel refreshes with its row bolded.


Read the comparison table

Read the comparison table in the facts panel: the selected body's row is bolded among all five, listing diameter, distance from the Sun, orbital period, and region for each.


Compare two worlds back to back

Compare two worlds back to back by clicking Ceres then Eris - the table stays on screen the whole time, so a diameter of about 940 km sits right next to one of 2,326 km for a direct read.
