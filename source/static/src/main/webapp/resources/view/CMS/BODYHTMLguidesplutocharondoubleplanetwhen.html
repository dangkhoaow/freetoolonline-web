When to Use Pluto-Charon Double Planet 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

Open the Pluto-Charon Double Planet 3D Explorer when you need a mutual tidal-lock lesson with an outside-primary barycenter - without a gravity-integrator desk.


An outside-barycenter lesson

An outside-barycenter lesson starts when you show that ~2,110 km from Pluto's center exceeds the 1188.3 km mean radius, so the green marker sits in empty space between the worlds.


Mass, period, and lock figures from New Horizons / JPL

Published figures land cleanly here: radii 1188.3 km / 606 km, masses 1.303e22 kg / 1.586e21 kg (~8.2:1), separation ~19,591 km, period 6.387 days, both tidally locked.


Sessions this explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are an Earth-Moon interior-barycenter deep-dive or a multi-body dwarf-planet size lineup. Use the Earth-Moon Barycenter 3D Explorer or the Dwarf Planet Comparison 3D Explorer for those sibling tasks. Walk the controls in the step-by-step guide.
