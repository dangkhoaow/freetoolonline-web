When to Use the Torino Impact Hazard Scale 3D Explorer - Best Sessions and Limits

Last reviewed: 2026-07-24

Open Torino Impact Hazard Scale 3D Explorer when you want to see how the same scale rated Apophis very differently in 2004 and 2006, or to compare its highest-ever rating against 2023 DW's lower one - not to check today's live CNEOS Sentry risk list or run a to-scale orbital-dynamics projection.


An Apophis-then-and-now lesson

A lesson starts on the Apophis Dec 2004 case to see the Torino 4 rating and its Yellow beacon, then switches to Apophis refined Aug 2006 to see the same object drop to Torino 0 and a White beacon once a longer observed arc removed the risk - the scale itself did not change, the knowledge behind it did.


Sessions this explorer does not fit

Sessions this page does not fit are checking the single 2013 impact that struck with no advance warning at all - use the Chelyabinsk Meteor 3D Explorer for that - or the geometry of Apophis's one specific 2029 close approach - use the Apophis 2029 Flyby 3D Explorer.
