Parallax Distance 3D Explorer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

See how Earth's 1 AU orbit baseline turns a tiny parallax angle into stellar distance - d (pc) = 1/p (arcsec), with 1 pc about 3.26 ly (about 206,265 AU) and Proxima Centauri at about 0.7687 arcsec.

Drag to orbit the view, scroll or pinch to zoom, and try Proxima, 1 arcsec, and 0.5 arcsec presets. Formula and Earth baseline buttons fill the facts panel.

The facts panel lists d = 1/p, 1 pc about 3.26 ly = about 206,265 AU, and Proxima Centauri parallax about 0.7687 arcsec.

  • Sun plus Earth at opposite orbit ends as the teaching baseline
  • Sight lines to a distant star that move with parallax presets
  • Proxima Centauri preset at about 0.7687 arcsec
  • 1 arcsec = 1 parsec and 0.5 arcsec = 2 pc literacy buttons
  • Distinct from Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer and Milky Way Map 3D Explorer
  • Runs fully in the browser with the vendored three.js engine - no account, no upload

Teachers use it to show why one arcsecond defines one parsec, students scrub Proxima versus 1 arcsec, and curious readers connect a tiny angle on the sky to a light-year distance without opening a catalog alone.

Everything renders on your device with WebGL. The 3D engine loads once (about 0.7 MB) and is cached; no scene data is sent to a server.

The scene is an educational visualization - sight-line angles are exaggerated so the triangle stays readable. It is not a Hipparcos reduction pipeline or a precise ephemeris.

For a step-by-step walkthrough, read the Parallax Distance 3D Explorer step-by-step guide. The Space 3D collection also includes a Stellar Magnitude Scale Explorer for brightness scales and a Milky Way Map 3D Explorer for galactic structure.
