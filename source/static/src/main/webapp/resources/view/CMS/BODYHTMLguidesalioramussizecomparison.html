How the Alioramus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Alioramus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 5.5 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside an Alioramus

What a person would see is a slender long-snouted tyrannosaurid whose panel length is about 5.5 m - roughly three body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 2.0 m high in the panel reconstruction. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the snout to feel how Alioramini differs from the bulkier Tarbosaurus outline.


What the Alioramus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a confirmed adult census: no confirmed adult specimens are known, so length and mass stay within published estimates (panel about 5.5 m / 500 kg, with disclosed ranges).
