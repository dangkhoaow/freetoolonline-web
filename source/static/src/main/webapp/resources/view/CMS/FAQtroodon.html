Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Troodon called the smartest dinosaur?

Because fossils show it had one of the largest brain-to-body-size ratios of any known dinosaur, alongside large, forward-set eyes. This does not mean it was as intelligent as a modern mammal - only that its brain was unusually large for its size among dinosaurs.

How big was Troodon?

Published estimates vary by source, roughly 1-3 m long and 10-50 kg. The panel uses commonly cited middle-of-range figures: about 2.4 m long and 50 kg. Turn on the human figure for scale.

Is "Troodon" a valid dinosaur name?

It is debated. The name was based on a single tooth found in 1855, so for decades other troodontid fossils from the same region were sometimes placed in separate genera (Stenonychosaurus, Latenivenatrix, Pectinodon). A 2025 study proposed a new reference specimen to keep the name Troodon formosus, which this page follows.

What did Troodon eat?

Its diet is debated. The serrated teeth resemble those of meat-eating dinosaurs, but their shape also resembles some plant-eating dinosaurs' teeth, so many researchers consider Troodon likely omnivorous.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

Size and era follow published figures, but feather color and pattern are artistic. This is not a fossil-accurate skeleton - fossils do not preserve original skin or feather color.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
