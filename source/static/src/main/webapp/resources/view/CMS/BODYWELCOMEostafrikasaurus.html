This page renders Ostafrikasaurus as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click the head, a limb, or the tail to read a fact about that part.

Ostafrikasaurus crassiserratus is a spinosaurid theropod from the Tendaguru Formation of Tanzania. Eric Buffetaut named it in 2012 from isolated teeth collected during early twentieth-century German expeditions. The holotype tooth is about 46 mm long and has unusually coarse serrations for a spinosaurid. No skull, limb, or vertebral bones are referred to the genus, so any full-body length or mass figure is a speculative reconstruction, not a published skeletal measurement.
