How the Hybodus size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Hybodus model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue about 2 m panel baseline. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Hybodus

What a person would see is an Early Jurassic hybodont shark - not a dinosaur - whose panel length is about 2 m - slightly longer than a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with body height about 0.5 m in the panel silhouette. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the snout to feel how a wastebasket-genus hybodont compares to you.


What the Hybodus size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single tight mass census: mass stays poorly constrained with secondary estimates about 45-90 kg and only the panel baseline 70 kg disclosed - the viewer does not claim mass is tightly constrained from fossils. Soft-tissue color in the viewer is still an artistic reconstruction. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.
