Archaeopteryx 3D Viewer

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21

This page renders an Archaeopteryx as a 3D model you can spin in the browser - drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person beside it for scale, and click a wing, the snout, or the long bony tail to read a fact about that part.

Archaeopteryx lived in the Late Jurassic, about 149 to 145 million years ago, in what is now Solnhofen, Germany. The Natural History Museum lists length about 0.5 m; mass estimates commonly fall about 0.5 to 1 kg. Hermann von Meyer named it in 1861. Feathered wings, teeth, and a long bony tail make it central to early-bird debates - and it is a dinosaur, not a pterosaur.
