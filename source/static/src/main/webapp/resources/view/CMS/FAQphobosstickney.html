Frequently Asked Questions

How big is Stickney compared with Phobos?

Stickney is about 9 kilometers across on a moon only about 27 by 22 by 18 kilometers, so the crater spans a large fraction of one face.

Who was Stickney named for?

Chloe Angeline Stickney Hall, mathematician and wife of Asaph Hall, who discovered Phobos and Deimos in 1877.

What are the grooves?

Long troughs and crater chains typically 100-200 meters wide and about 20 meters deep. Hypotheses include Stickney impact stress, ejecta from Mars impacts, and tidal forces - this page does not pick one.

Is Phobos falling toward Mars?

Yes, slowly - NASA cites roughly 1.8 meters closer every hundred years; in tens of millions of years it may hit Mars or break into a ring.

Is the shape exact?

No. The real body is more irregular; the scene uses a smooth ellipsoid so Stickney and the grooves stay readable.

Does this include Deimos?

No. This explorer focuses on Phobos and Stickney only.

Is this a physics simulation?

No. It is an educational visualization with compressed scales and schematic grooves, not an impact or tidal solver.
