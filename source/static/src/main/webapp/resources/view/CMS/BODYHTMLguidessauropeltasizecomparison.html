How the Sauropelta size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Sauropelta model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to the catalogue 5.5 m panel baseline (within the published about 5.2-6 m range). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Sauropelta

What a person would see is an armored nodosaurid whose panel length is about 5.5 m - roughly three body lengths of a 1.8 m person laid end to end - with hips about 1.5 m high in the panel. Turn on Size vs human and orbit beside the neck spines to feel how the armored body sits next to the scale figure.


What the Sauropelta size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a single invented length outside the published 5.2-6 m range, and they do not invent a tail club - nodosaurids lack that ankylosaurid trait.
