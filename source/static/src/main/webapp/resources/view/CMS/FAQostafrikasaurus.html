Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Ostafrikasaurus?

Body length and mass are not known. The taxon rests on isolated teeth; the holotype crown is about 46 mm long (Buffetaut 2012). Any full-body size shown here is a speculative reconstruction for scale, not a published skeletal measurement. Turn on the human figure to compare a person with that reconstruction, not with a measured skeleton.

What kind of dinosaur was Ostafrikasaurus?

It is treated as a spinosaurid theropod - a long-snouted carnivore group that includes Spinosaurus and Baryonyx - based on tooth shape. The teeth have unusually coarse serrations compared with many later spinosaurids. No postcranial bones are referred to the genus.

When and where did Ostafrikasaurus live?

The teeth come from the Tendaguru Formation in what is now Lindi Region, Tanzania. They were collected during German East Africa expeditions between about 1909 and 1912. Eric Buffetaut named Ostafrikasaurus crassiserratus in 2012. The precise age of the tooth-bearing levels around the Late Jurassic to earliest Cretaceous boundary is still debated.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

No full skeleton exists for this taxon. The on-screen body is a speculative spinosaurid reconstruction used so you can explore the animal in 3D. Skin color and soft tissue are artistic. The securely published figure on this page is the holotype tooth length of about 46 mm; body length and mass are not presented as measured facts.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
