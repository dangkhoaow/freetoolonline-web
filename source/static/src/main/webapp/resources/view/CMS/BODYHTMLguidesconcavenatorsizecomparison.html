How the Concavenator size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Concavenator model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio used by the viewer (6.0 m). Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker. Published length still spans about 5-6 m (NHM 6.0 m), so the toggle picks one clear scale inside that disclosed range.


What a person would see beside a Concavenator

What a person would see is a Spanish Early Cretaceous carcharodontosaur with a pointed crest of tall vertebrae near the hips - turn on the person in the viewer and orbit beside a hind leg to feel how the horizontal theropod posture sits relative to the scale figure. A license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in after first paint; soft-tissue color remains an artistic choice if the model load fails. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. This guide does not claim the hip crest was definitely a camel-style fat hump.


What the Concavenator size numbers are not

What these size numbers are not is a claim of one precise length or weight: the Concavenator size comparison discloses the about 5-6 m / 320-400 kg ranges (with larger popular accounts noted) and uses 6.0 m only for the Size vs human scale; it does not claim a definite camel-style fat hump, does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, and this is not a win/lose game.
