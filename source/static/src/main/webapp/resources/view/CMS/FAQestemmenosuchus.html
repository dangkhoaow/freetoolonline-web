Frequently Asked Questions

How big was a real Estemmenosuchus?

Estemmenosuchus mirabilis grew to about 3 m long. Published weight estimates vary widely, from roughly 200 kg to over a tonne. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a person.

Was Estemmenosuchus a dinosaur?

No. Estemmenosuchus was a therapsid - a mammal-like reptile more closely related to the ancestors of mammals than to dinosaurs. It lived during the Permian period, tens of millions of years before the first dinosaurs appeared.

What were the bony crowns on its head for?

Most researchers think the paired, antler-like bony crowns on the skull were used for display or head-to-head combat between rivals, similar to how some modern horned animals use theirs, rather than for anything crocodile-like despite the genus name.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The body proportions follow a fossil skeleton found at the Ezhovo site in Russia, and some specimens even preserve impressions of the animal's wrinkled, bumpy skin. Even so, the exact skin color shown here is an artistic reconstruction - fossils do not preserve pigment. The length, era, and description figures shown are real published values.

When and where did it live?

Roughly 269 to 265 million years ago, during the Wordian age of the Middle Permian, at what is now the Ezhovo fossil site near the town of Ochyor in Russia's Perm region.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
