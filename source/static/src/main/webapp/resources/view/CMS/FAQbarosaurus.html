Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Barosaurus?

Published figures put an adult Barosaurus at about 24 to 27 m long and about 12 to 20 tonnes. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

How is Barosaurus different from Diplodocus?

The two are close relatives, but Barosaurus had a longer neck - 16 neck vertebrae to Diplodocus's 15, with some individual neck bones up to 50 percent longer - and a proportionally shorter tail.

Could Barosaurus really rear up on its hind legs?

A well-known mounted display at the American Museum of Natural History shows Barosaurus reared up to defend a juvenile from a predator, and some sources suggest this could put its head roughly 15 m high. This is a debated behavioral inference, not a directly measured fact.

When and where did Barosaurus live?

In the Late Jurassic, around 150 to 145 million years ago, mainly in what is now the western United States. Othniel Charles Marsh named the genus in 1890 from fossils found in 1889 near Mission, South Dakota, and excavated by John Bell Hatcher.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
