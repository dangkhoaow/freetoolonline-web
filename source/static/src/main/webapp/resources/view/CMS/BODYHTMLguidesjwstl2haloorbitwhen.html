When to use JWST L2 Halo Orbit 3D Explorer

Use this explorer when you want to show why Webb sits at the Sun-Earth L2 point instead of orbiting Earth directly, and how a halo path keeps it out of Earth's and the Moon's shadow.

  • Teaching why L2 lets one sunshield block the Sun, Earth, and Moon at once
  • Explaining the difference between orbiting a planet and orbiting an unstable Lagrange point
  • Showing why Webb needs station-keeping burns roughly every 21 days

This is an educational halo-path approximation, not a live tracker or a precomputed ephemeris.
