How to Explore Venus Runaway Greenhouse Explorer 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-23

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Open the Venus Runaway Greenhouse Explorer 3D, drag the CO2 slider or press Reset, and see why a thick CO2 blanket keeps Venus hotter than Mercury, even though Venus orbits almost twice as far from the Sun.

  1. Open /space-3d/venus-runaway-greenhouse.html and wait for Venus to render.
  2. Drag the CO2 slider away from 96% and watch the haze shell around Venus thin out and change color.
  3. Press "Reset" to snap the slider back to Venus's real measured composition (96.5% CO2) and read the real 464 C / 737 K surface temperature in the facts panel.
  4. Turn on the Mercury-comparison toggle to see a to-scale Mercury sphere next to Venus, then read why Mercury - almost twice as close to the Sun - is still colder on average.
  5. Turn on the pressure-comparison bars to see Venus's 92-bar surface pressure next to Earth's 1 bar and Mercury's near-zero pressure.
  6. Read the facts panel for the full reference table of every published figure used on this page.
