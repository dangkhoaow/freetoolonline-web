How the Tyrannosaurus rex size-vs-human toggle stays honest

The Tyrannosaurus rex model is drawn to a fixed on-screen length so it fits the canvas; the Size vs human control then places a 1.8 m person at the true length ratio to that real ~12.3 m figure. Tap Size vs human under the canvas to show or hide the scale figure - the comparison is proportional, not a decorative sticker.


What a person would see beside a Tyrannosaurus rex

What a person would see is a hip joint towering overhead: at about 3.7 m at the hips, a Tyrannosaurus rex hip joint sits well above a 1.8 m person's head. From snout to tail the animal spans roughly 12.3 m - turn on the person in the viewer and stand the camera beside a hind leg to feel how far the back is above eye level.
