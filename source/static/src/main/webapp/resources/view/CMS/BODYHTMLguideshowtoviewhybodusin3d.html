How to View Hybodus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Hybodus 3D Viewer runs an Early Jurassic hybodont shark in your browser - not a dinosaur - length about 2 m, mass poorly constrained. Drag, zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person, and click a body region.


Open the Hybodus page and take the camera

Open the Hybodus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Hybodus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural marine silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Hybodus controls under the canvas

Under the Hybodus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio used by the viewer (about 2 m)

Read the Hybodus facts panel and click a body part

The Hybodus facts panel lists length about 2 m typical (panel baseline 2 m; up to about 3 m for large H. hauffianus), height about 0.5 m (panel silhouette), mass poorly constrained with secondary estimates about 45-90 kg (panel baseline 70 kg), the Early Jurassic window about 201-175 million years ago in Europe for core species, carnivore / durophage diet (heterodont teeth), wastebasket genus historically with type H. reticulatus, and that Hybodus was a hybodont shark - not a dinosaur. Heterodont teeth and dorsal fin spines are distinctive hybodont traits. A short click - not a drag - on a body region surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. When the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Hybodus how-to guide is not

The Hybodus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not claim Hybodus was a dinosaur, it does not claim mass is tightly constrained from fossils, it does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, it does not invent game score or win states, and it does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
