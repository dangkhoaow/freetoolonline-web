When the Mosasaurus browser viewer fits

Pick the Mosasaurus browser viewer when you want drag-orbit on a water plane, Size vs human against the catalogue ~17 m length within the published 11-17 m range, and part clicks on the head, a flipper, or the tail without installing anything. The engine lazy-loads after paint, then stays cached - good for a classroom laptop or a quick desktop check of the Late Cretaceous marine carnivore numbers (82-66 Mya, first fossils near Maastricht in 1764).


When a phone AR app or museum fits better

A phone AR app wins when you need the swimming silhouette on your living-room floor; this viewer never claims room placement. A museum wins for walking beside mounted mosasaur bone and feeling hall-scale presence the screen cannot match. A static image wins only when you need a single shareable frame with no interactivity.


What this Mosasaurus comparison is not

This Mosasaurus comparison is a trade-off table for the shipped viewer - it is not a ranking of commercial AR brands and it does not invent AR features the page does not have.
