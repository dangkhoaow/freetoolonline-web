Frequently Asked Questions

How big was Brachiosaurus?

About 22 m long and about 46.9 tonnes according to the Natural History Museum. Head height in common reconstructions is about 9.4 m. Turn on the human figure in the viewer to see the scale against a 1.8 m person.

Why were the front legs longer?

Longer front legs raised the chest and neck, letting the animal reach high foliage. That upright silhouette is the trait the name Brachiosaurus ("arm lizard") points to.

When and where did Brachiosaurus live?

In the Late Jurassic, about 152 to 145 million years ago, mainly in what is now the western United States. Elmer S. Riggs named the genus in 1903 from Colorado finds (Brachiosaurus altithorax).

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow published figures, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The length, weight, and age figures shown are real published values from the Natural History Museum.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
