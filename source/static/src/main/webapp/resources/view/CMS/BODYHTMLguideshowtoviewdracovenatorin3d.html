How to View Dracovenator in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Dracovenator 3D Viewer runs an Early Jurassic South African basal neotheropod - length about 5.5-6.5 m (panel 6.0 m), mass about 250 kg. Drag, zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person, and click a body region.


Open the Dracovenator page and take the camera

Open the Dracovenator 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Dracovenator with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural theropod silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Dracovenator controls under the canvas

Under the Dracovenator canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio used by the viewer (6.0 m within the about 5.5-6.5 m published range)

Read the Dracovenator facts panel and click a body part

The Dracovenator facts panel lists length about 5.5-6.5 m (panel baseline 6.0 m), hip height about 1.5-2.0 m (panel 1.8 m), mass about 250 kg (commonly cited), the Early Jurassic Hettangian window about 201-199 million years ago in the upper Elliot Formation of the Eastern Cape, South Africa, carnivore diet, and naming from Yates 2005 (type material collected by Kitching and Huma). A short click - not a drag - on a body region surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. When the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton - only skull material is known.


What the Dracovenator how-to guide is not

The Dracovenator how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), does not invent a single length outside the published 5.5-6.5 m range, does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, does not invent game score or win states, and does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
