How to View Tarbosaurus in 3D

Last reviewed: 2026-07-20

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

Tarbosaurus 3D Viewer runs a large Asian tyrannosaurid, a close relative of Tyrannosaurus, in your browser. Drag to rotate, scroll or pinch to zoom, toggle a 1.8 m person for scale, and click a body region for a fossil-sourced fact.


Open the Tarbosaurus page and take the camera

Open the Tarbosaurus 3D Viewer and wait for the facts panel to fill. When the status line says you can drag, orbit Tarbosaurus with the pointer and zoom with the scroll wheel or a two-finger pinch. Fullscreen expands the wrapper so the controls stay visible beside the scene. After first paint a license-clean CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF may swap in automatically from the CDN; if that load fails the page stays on the procedural theropod silhouette with zero regression.


Use the four Tarbosaurus controls under the canvas

Under the Tarbosaurus canvas, four controls handle view and scale:

  • Fullscreen - fills the display while keeping the buttons visible
  • Stop rotation / Auto-rotate - freezes or resumes auto-spin
  • Idle motion - adds subtle body sway; tap again for Stand still
  • Size vs human - shows a 1.8 m person at the honest panel length ratio used by the viewer (about 10.5 m)

Read the Tarbosaurus facts panel and click a body part

The Tarbosaurus facts panel lists length about 10-12 m (panel baseline 10.5 m), height about 3.5 m (panel silhouette), mass about 4-5 t (panel baseline 4.5 t / 4500 kg), the Late Cretaceous window about 70-66 million years ago in the Nemegt Formation of Mongolia, carnivore diet, and naming from Maleev in 1955. Tarbosaurus was a large Asian tyrannosaurid, a close relative of Tyrannosaurus - a distinct species, not the same animal. A short click - not a drag - on a body region surfaces a short fossil fact. Soft-tissue color is an artistic reconstruction. When the CC-BY-SA 4.0 glTF by seth the yutyrannus via Printables loads, the status text notes a real model with that credit - a stylized reconstruction, not a laser scan. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.


What the Tarbosaurus how-to guide is not

The Tarbosaurus how-to guide is a controls walkthrough for the in-browser viewer only - it does not place the model in your room (no AR), it does not conflate Tarbosaurus with Tyrannosaurus, it does not invent a single mass outside the published 4-5 t range, it does not invent soft-tissue color accuracy, it does not invent game score or win states, and it does not claim the optional glTF is a scientifically exact reconstruction.
