Lunar Eclipse 3D Explorer vs Moon Phases 3D and Solar Eclipse

Reviewed by FreetoolOnline editorial team

The Lunar Eclipse 3D Explorer shows lunar-eclipse shadow cones in one page load - 0 MB, USD 0, no account. Moon Phases 3D scrubs a synodic month but adds no Earth's-shadow cones; a separate Solar Eclipse page for the Moon's umbra on Earth is not shipped yet.

Where Lunar Eclipse wins

Where Lunar Eclipse wins: Everything runs locally with WebGL. For teaching Earth's penumbra and umbra cones, Danjon totality color, and published figures - maximum totality up to 107 minutes, node alignment required - it is the focused route. The progress slider and Danjon L0-L4 buttons sit on one eclipse lesson; Moon Phases 3D keeps the synodic month and everyday lit hemisphere.


Where Moon Phases 3D wins

Moon Phases 3D Explorer wins when you need everyday phase names across a 29.53-day synodic month - new moon through full moon and back - driven by the Sun-Earth-Moon angle. It does not add shadow cones or Danjon totality color.


Where Solar Eclipse will fit

A future Solar Eclipse 3D Explorer will show the Moon's shadow falling on Earth at new moon - the opposite geometry from a lunar eclipse. Until that page ships, use Lunar Eclipse for Earth's shadow on the Moon and Moon Phases 3D for phase geometry literacy.


A reasonable rule

As a reasonable rule, use Lunar Eclipse for shadow-cone and Danjon totality literacy in under a minute; use Moon Phases 3D for the synodic month and everyday phases; watch for the planned Solar Eclipse page for the Moon's umbra on Earth; use an almanac or planetarium app for the date of the next real eclipse. The NASA figures in the panel - up to 107 minutes totality, Danjon L0-L4, node alignment - stay accurate either way.
