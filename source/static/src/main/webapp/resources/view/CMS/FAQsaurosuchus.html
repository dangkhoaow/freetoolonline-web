Frequently Asked Questions

Was Saurosuchus a dinosaur?

No. Saurosuchus was a rauisuchian pseudosuchian - a crocodile-line archosaur from the Late Triassic - not a dinosaur. This viewer includes it because it was the apex predator of an ecosystem that also contained some of the earliest known dinosaurs.

How big was Saurosuchus?

Published estimates span about 5.5 to 7 m long, with some studies suggesting up to 9 m because no complete skeleton is known. Mass is estimated at about 590 kg, following a three-dimensional model published by Henderson and Snively in 2003. Turn on the human figure to compare with a person.

Did Saurosuchus live alongside dinosaurs?

Yes. Its fossils come from the Ischigualasto Formation in Argentina, which also preserves some of the earliest known dinosaurs, including Herrerasaurus and Eoraptor. Saurosuchus was almost certainly the apex predator of that ecosystem, well before dinosaurs became dominant.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions follow the same cautious quadrupedal rauisuchian reconstruction as this site's Postosuchus viewer - a deep boxy skull, a raised trunk, and legs tucked more under the body than a sprawled crocodilian - but the skin color and pattern are an artistic reconstruction. Fossils preserve bone, not soft tissue or color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton.

Where and when was Saurosuchus found?

Its holotype skull was found in 1957 by Galileo J. Scaglia and Leocadio Soria in the Ischigualasto Formation near San Juan, Argentina, and described by Osvaldo A. Reig in 1959. It lived roughly 231 to 225 million years ago, in the Late Triassic.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached. When available, a free-licensed glTF model may swap in after first paint.
