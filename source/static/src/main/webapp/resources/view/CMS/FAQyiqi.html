Frequently Asked Questions

Was Yi qi a pterosaur or a bat?

No. Yi qi was a dinosaur - specifically a scansoriopterygid, a small group of dinosaurs related to early birds. Its wing membrane, supported by a rod-like wrist bone, worked on the same basic principle as a bat's or pterosaur's wing, but the animal itself was a true dinosaur.

How big was Yi qi?

Tiny. Wingspan is commonly cited at about 60 cm, and the skull was under 4 cm long. Published weight estimates vary from roughly 0.38 to 0.52 kg across different sources, often rounded to under 0.4 kg - about the weight of a large pigeon. When and where did Yi qi live?

In the Middle to Late Jurassic, roughly 166 to 159 million years ago, in what is now Hebei Province, China. The single known fossil was found in the Tiaojishan Formation near Mutoudeng and was named and described in 2015 by Xu Xing, Zheng Xiaoting, Corwin Sullivan and colleagues in the journal Nature.

What did Yi qi eat?

The exact diet was not directly preserved. Its short, deep skull and small, forward-pointing teeth suggest it hunted insects, likely up in tree branches, but this is an inference from the skull shape rather than a directly observed fact.

Is the model scientifically accurate?

The proportions and wing shape follow the published description, but the skin color and soft-tissue outline are an artistic reconstruction - fossils preserve bone and membrane outline, not color. This model is not a fossil-accurate skeleton. The wingspan, weight range, and age figures shown are real published values.

Do I need to install anything to view it?

No. The model renders in your browser with WebGL - no app, no account, and nothing about your visit is sent to a server. The 3D engine loads once and is then cached.
